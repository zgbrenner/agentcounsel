## Stop and Ask — Missing Required Inputs

The skill cannot proceed without required gate information. The jurisdiction and governing law applicable to the policy are not provided, and one or more of the skill's required inputs — the RWI policy/quote package, the purchase agreement, and the client's posture — are also not provided `[CONFIRM: jurisdiction and governing law]`. Building a coverage-to-representations map or an exclusions register without the policy, the purchase agreement, and the client's posture would mean inventing policy terms and rep language. This skill stops here before any substantive review to avoid fabricating a mapping from missing documents.

## Inputs Required Before Substantive Work

- [ ] The RWI policy or quote package — binder, policy form, and exclusions schedule, in full.
- [ ] The purchase agreement whose representations the policy is meant to cover.
- [ ] The client's posture: buyer/insured, seller, or the coordinating deal team.
- [ ] Jurisdiction and governing law of the policy `[verify jurisdiction]`.

## What This Skill Will Not Do

- Will not assume policy terms, exclusions, or limits to build a coverage-to-representations map.
- Will not assume the client's posture to fill the gap.
- Will not assume a jurisdiction or governing law.
- Will not conclude that any matter is or is not covered, in any circumstance — that determination requires the actual policy text and is never within this skill's scope regardless of inputs provided.
