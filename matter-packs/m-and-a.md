# M&A Matter Packs

Workflow bundles for the Mergers & Acquisitions practice area — recommended
sequences of AgentCounsel skills for common M&A matters. See
`matter-packs/README.md` for what a matter pack is and how to use one.

These packs are process guidance, not legal advice. Every skill they sequence
produces draft work product that a qualified, licensed attorney must review.
Before starting, read the `core/` operating rules and
`practice-profiles/m-and-a.md`, and confirm the jurisdiction, deal type, the
side (buyer, seller, company, investor, or target), the transaction stage, and
the document set. None of these skills decides whether a provision is binding,
states tax, securities, antitrust, or employment law, computes a deadline,
approves signing or closing, or reaches a legal conclusion — those remain
attorney work.

Each pack ends with **handoff notes** — what the output of one step contributes
to the next. The handoffs are passed by the human running the pack: an output
is reviewed, then carried forward as an input or as context.

## 1. Buyer-Side Acquisition Matter Pack

**When to use.** Representing the buyer in an acquisition, from the letter of
intent through diligence, agreement negotiation, closing, and integration.

**Required starting materials.** The LOI or term sheet; the deal type and
structure; the target profile and industry; the jurisdiction; and the diligence
document set and draft agreements as they are produced.

**Recommended skill sequence.**

| Step | Skill | Produces |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `skills/m-and-a/loi-term-sheet-review/SKILL.md` | A buyer-side review of the LOI or term sheet |
| 2 | `skills/m-and-a/acquisition-diligence-request-list/SKILL.md` | A tailored diligence request list by workstream |
| 3 | `skills/m-and-a/data-room-index-review/SKILL.md` | A data-room gap matrix and follow-up requests |
| 4 | `skills/m-and-a/purchase-agreement-issue-list/SKILL.md` | A buyer-side issue list and risk matrix on the agreement |
| 5 | `skills/m-and-a/reps-warranties-disclosure-schedule-review/SKILL.md` | A rep-by-rep review against the disclosure schedules |
| 6 | `skills/m-and-a/indemnity-escrow-risk-review/SKILL.md` | An indemnity and escrow architecture and risk review |
| 7 | `skills/m-and-a/third-party-consents-assignment-review/SKILL.md` | A consent and change-of-control tracker |
| 8 | `skills/m-and-a/closing-deliverables-tracker/SKILL.md` | A closing-deliverables tracker |
| 9 | `skills/m-and-a/post-closing-obligations-tracker/SKILL.md` | A post-closing obligation tracker |
| 10 | `skills/m-and-a/integration-legal-issues-checklist/SKILL.md` | A legal integration checklist |

**Expected outputs.** A reviewed LOI; a diligence request list and data-room
gap analysis; an issue list and risk matrix on the purchase agreement; a
disclosure-schedule review; an indemnity and escrow review; a consents tracker;
and closing, post-closing, and integration trackers.

**Attorney verification checkpoints.**
- After step 1 — confirm the binding status of LOI provisions before signing.
- After steps 4–6 — confirm the issue priorities, the disclosure-schedule
  findings, and the indemnity allocation before signing.
- After step 7 — confirm which consents are required and how they are obtained.
- Before signing, closing, recording, sending notices, making disclosures, or
  taking board or shareholder action.

**Handoff notes.** The reviewed LOI (step 1) frames the deal terms the
diligence list (step 2) should probe. The data-room gap matrix (step 3) feeds
the diligence list's follow-up requests and surfaces the documents the
agreement and schedule reviews (steps 4–5) will need. The agreement issue list
(step 4) orients the disclosure-schedule review (step 5) and the indemnity
review (step 6). Diligence findings carried into step 5 let the review flag
mismatches between known facts and the disclosures. Contracts surfaced in
diligence and in step 5 feed the consent review (step 7). The agreement and its
ancillaries feed the closing (step 8) and post-closing (step 9) trackers; the
post-closing tracker and the consent review feed the integration checklist
(step 10).

**Do not use when.** The matter is a sale from the seller's side (use Matter
Pack 2); or it is a single, self-contained review — route that to the one skill
via `WORKFLOW_ROUTER.md`.

## 2. Seller-Side Exit Readiness Matter Pack

**When to use.** Representing the seller or target preparing for and running a
sale process — getting the company ready for diligence, then negotiating to a
close.

**Required starting materials.** The target's corporate, financial, and
contract records; the deal type; the jurisdiction; and the LOI and draft
agreements as the process develops.

**Recommended skill sequence.**

| Step | Skill | Produces |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `skills/m-and-a/acquisition-diligence-request-list/SKILL.md` | A diligence request list, used to anticipate what a buyer will ask for |
| 2 | `skills/m-and-a/data-room-index-review/SKILL.md` | A data-room gap matrix for the seller's own data room |
| 3 | `skills/m-and-a/loi-term-sheet-review/SKILL.md` | A seller-side review of the LOI or term sheet |
| 4 | `skills/m-and-a/reps-warranties-disclosure-schedule-review/SKILL.md` | A review of the representations against the seller's disclosure schedules |
| 5 | `skills/m-and-a/purchase-agreement-issue-list/SKILL.md` | A seller-side issue list and risk matrix |
| 6 | `skills/m-and-a/indemnity-escrow-risk-review/SKILL.md` | A seller-side indemnity and escrow review |
| 7 | `skills/m-and-a/closing-deliverables-tracker/SKILL.md` | A closing-deliverables tracker |
| 8 | `skills/m-and-a/post-closing-obligations-tracker/SKILL.md` | A post-closing obligation tracker |

**Expected outputs.** A diligence-readiness request list and data-room gap
analysis; a seller-side LOI review; a disclosure-schedule review; a seller-side
issue list, risk matrix, and indemnity review; and closing and post-closing
trackers.

**Attorney verification checkpoints.**
- After step 2 — confirm the data room is complete and privilege is preserved.
- After step 4 — confirm the disclosure schedules before they are delivered.
- After steps 5–6 — confirm the seller's positions on the agreement and the
  indemnity package before signing.
- Before signing, closing, or making any disclosure.

**Handoff notes.** Steps 1–2 are run against the seller's own materials to find
gaps before a buyer does; the gap matrix drives what the seller assembles. The
seller-side LOI review (step 3) sets the positions the agreement review (step 5)
defends. The disclosure-schedule review (step 4) is run early on the seller
side so schedule gaps are closed before delivery; its findings inform step 5.
Steps 5–6 set the seller's negotiating posture. The agreement and ancillaries
feed the closing and post-closing trackers (steps 7–8).

**Do not use when.** The matter is buyer-side (use Matter Pack 1); or the focus
is solely the disclosure schedules (use Matter Pack 3).

## 3. Disclosure Schedule Review Matter Pack

**When to use.** A focused review of an acquisition agreement against its
disclosure schedules — for example a pre-signing schedule-completeness pass.

**Required starting materials.** The purchase agreement (with its
representations article); the disclosure schedules; the side; and any known
diligence facts.

**Recommended skill sequence.**

| Step | Skill | Produces |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `skills/m-and-a/purchase-agreement-issue-list/SKILL.md` | An issue list on the agreement, orienting the schedule review |
| 2 | `skills/m-and-a/reps-warranties-disclosure-schedule-review/SKILL.md` | A rep-by-rep review against the disclosure schedules |
| 3 | `skills/m-and-a/third-party-consents-assignment-review/SKILL.md` | A consent tracker for contracts surfaced in the schedules |
| 4 | `skills/m-and-a/closing-deliverables-tracker/SKILL.md` | A closing-deliverables tracker |

**Expected outputs.** An agreement issue list; a rep-by-rep disclosure-schedule
review with completeness and unresolved-item flags; a consent tracker; and a
closing-deliverables tracker.

**Attorney verification checkpoints.**
- After step 2 — confirm every "to be provided" item and every schedule gap;
  the skill flags adequacy, it does not conclude on it.
- After step 3 — confirm which consents are required.
- Before the schedules are delivered or the agreement is signed.

**Handoff notes.** The agreement issue list (step 1) identifies which
representations matter most, focusing the schedule review (step 2). The contract
schedules reviewed in step 2 — material contracts, leases, IP licenses — are the
input set for the consent review (step 3). Consents and schedule items that
must be resolved by closing feed the closing-deliverables tracker (step 4).

**Do not use when.** The matter is a whole-deal engagement (use Matter Pack 1
or 2).

## 4. Signing-to-Closing Matter Pack

**When to use.** Driving a signed deal to closing and through the immediate
post-closing period — the interim covenant period, consents, and closing
mechanics.

**Required starting materials.** The signed purchase agreement and ancillary
documents; the deal type and structure; the side; and the contracts that may
require consent.

**Recommended skill sequence.**

| Step | Skill | Produces |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `skills/m-and-a/third-party-consents-assignment-review/SKILL.md` | A consent and change-of-control tracker |
| 2 | `skills/m-and-a/closing-deliverables-tracker/SKILL.md` | A closing-deliverables tracker |
| 3 | `skills/m-and-a/post-closing-obligations-tracker/SKILL.md` | A post-closing obligation tracker |
| 4 | `skills/m-and-a/integration-legal-issues-checklist/SKILL.md` | A legal integration checklist |

**Expected outputs.** A consent tracker; a closing-deliverables tracker; a
post-closing obligation tracker; and a legal integration checklist.

**Attorney verification checkpoints.**
- After step 1 — confirm which consents are required and the path to obtain
  them; the skill does not conclude on enforceability or whether consent is
  legally required.
- After step 2 — confirm every closing deliverable and every date; the skill
  flags dates, it never computes them.
- Before closing, recording, sending notices, or making regulatory filings.

**Handoff notes.** The consent tracker (step 1) feeds the closing-deliverables
tracker (step 2): consents that must be in hand at closing become closing
deliverables. The signed agreement and ancillaries feed the post-closing
tracker (step 3). The post-closing obligations and the consents feed the
integration checklist (step 4), which carries the legal threads forward past
closing.

**Do not use when.** The deal is not yet signed, or the agreement and ancillary
documents are not available — start with Matter Pack 1 or 2.

## 5. Data Room Readiness Matter Pack

**When to use.** Building or auditing a data room — typically seller-side or
company-side — before or early in a process, so diligence runs cleanly and the
disclosure schedules can be built from a complete record.

**Required starting materials.** The data room index or file list; the deal
type; the side; the target profile; and any representations article or draft
agreement available.

**Recommended skill sequence.**

| Step | Skill | Produces |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `skills/m-and-a/acquisition-diligence-request-list/SKILL.md` | A diligence request list — the standard against which the data room is measured |
| 2 | `skills/m-and-a/data-room-index-review/SKILL.md` | A data-room gap matrix and follow-up request list |
| 3 | `skills/m-and-a/reps-warranties-disclosure-schedule-review/SKILL.md` | A review tying the representations to the schedules the data room must support |
| 4 | `skills/m-and-a/third-party-consents-assignment-review/SKILL.md` | A consent tracker for contracts in the data room |

**Expected outputs.** A diligence request list; a data-room gap matrix with
stale, duplicate, and ambiguous-file flags and privilege concerns; a
disclosure-schedule review keyed to the data room; and a consent tracker.

**Attorney verification checkpoints.**
- After step 2 — confirm the gap findings and resolve every privilege or
  confidentiality concern before the data room is opened.
- After step 3 — confirm that the data room supports each disclosure schedule.
- Before the data room is made available to a counterparty.

**Handoff notes.** The diligence request list (step 1) is the checklist the
data-room review (step 2) measures the index against — gaps are items on the
list with no corresponding data-room document. The data-room contents and the
representations feed step 3, which confirms each schedule can be supported by
documents in the room. Contracts in the data room feed the consent review
(step 4). Note that `data-room-index-review` works only from the index — it
does not read document contents from filenames.

**Do not use when.** The data room contents (not just the index) need
substantive review — that is the agreement, schedule, and diligence work of
Matter Packs 1–3.
