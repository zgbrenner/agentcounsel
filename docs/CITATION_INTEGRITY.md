# Citation Integrity

Citation integrity is the structured review of legal authorities and citations
in draft legal work product. It helps an attorney see which citations are
user-provided, source-provided, model-suggested, unsupported, malformed, or
non-legal factual sources.

It is not automated legal citation verification. AgentCounsel does not claim
that a citation exists, is current, is binding, or supports a proposition
unless that has actually been verified from source material available in the
session. Final citation verification remains attorney work.

## Citation Classes

Use `skills/legal-methodology/citation-integrity-check/SKILL.md` to classify
each citation:

| Class | Meaning |
|---|---|
| User-provided authority | The user supplied the authority text or citation. |
| Source-provided authority | The authority appears in a source available in the session. |
| Model-suggested authority requiring verification | The model surfaced the authority and it must be independently checked. |
| Unsupported or unverifiable authority | The citation cannot be traced to available materials. |
| Non-legal factual source | The source supports a factual, not legal, proposition. |
| Citation format issue | The reference is incomplete, malformed, or missing required details. |

## Common Flags

- invented-looking case names
- invented or incomplete statutes
- missing pin cites
- unsupported propositions
- jurisdiction mismatch
- outdated authority risk
- failure to distinguish binding from persuasive authority
- quotations that cannot be checked against source text

## Relationship To Source Validation

Citation integrity focuses on legal authorities and citation form. Source
validation focuses on claim/source support across all material factual and
legal claims. High-risk research or adversarial communications often need both.
