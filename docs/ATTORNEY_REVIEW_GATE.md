# Attorney Review Gate

The attorney review gate is the final quality-control handoff before a legal
draft goes to a qualified attorney for review. It does not approve the draft
for reliance, sending, filing, or signature.

Use `skills/legal-methodology/attorney-review-gate/SKILL.md` when a high-risk
draft needs a consolidated readiness check.

## Gate Outputs

The gate produces:

- must-confirm facts
- must-confirm law
- must-confirm citations and sources
- client decision points
- ethics and professional-responsibility flags
- privilege and confidentiality flags
- jurisdiction and deadline flags
- ready for attorney review / not ready for attorney review status
- reasons for not-ready findings

## Status Meanings

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Ready for attorney review | The draft is organized enough for a lawyer to review with the listed confirmation items. |
| Not ready for attorney review | Missing inputs, unsupported authorities, unresolved assumptions, or confidentiality issues prevent meaningful attorney review. |

Neither status means the draft is final. A qualified attorney must review,
correct, and adopt the work before it is relied upon.
