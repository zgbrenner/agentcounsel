# Environmental and ESG Overlay Profile

A sector configuration profile, in the same style as the files under `practice-profiles/`. It records how a team's work in **environmental and ESG** differs from the general case.

This profile is **not** legal work product and **not** legal advice. It is internal configuration. It must be reviewed and approved by a supervising attorney — ideally an environmental / ESG-specialist attorney — before it is loaded alongside any skill. Organization-specific values are left as `[ORGANIZATION TO FILL]` placeholders because this profile ships with the overlay scaffold, not with a specific organization's configuration.

The overlay's framework references (`OVERLAY.md`) are paired with this profile. The profile sets organization-specific positions; the OVERLAY.md sets sector-wide tuning. Both are subordinate to `core/` and to the supervising attorney.

## Sector and scope

- **Sector / client type:** environmental and ESG — manufacturers and industrial operators, energy and chemicals companies, consumer-products companies making sustainability claims, public companies (and large private companies) within reach of climate- and sustainability-disclosure regimes, asset managers with ESG-labelled products, real-estate owners, developers, and lenders, importers with supply-chain diligence exposure, and the vendors and advisors serving any of the above.
- **In-scope activities:** regulatory, securities-disclosure, product-legal, contracts, and transactional-diligence work coloured by environmental permitting and compliance regimes, contaminated-property liability regimes, climate / ESG disclosure regimes (securities-style and CSRD-style), greenwashing / marketing-claims frameworks, supply-chain diligence regimes, and ESG fund-labelling frameworks `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **Out of scope:** specialist environmental work that routes to a separate practice — medium-specific permitting and permit negotiation, remediation strategy and cleanup-program negotiation, environmental litigation and citizen-suit defense, criminal-environmental defense, emissions accounting and technical verification, customs / trade substantive determinations, insurance-coverage disputes, and natural-resources / project-development work. The overlay surfaces these as routing flags; the work itself is performed by specialist counsel and technical consultants.

## Heightened-handling rules

The following data categories, populations, or document types require heightened confidentiality and care, in addition to general matter-file handling:

- **Release, spill, and incident records** — enforcement-sensitive; preserve, restrict access, and route to counsel before any external use.
- **Internal environmental-audit and self-assessment material** — audit-privilege and self-disclosure frameworks may attach `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`; route handling questions to counsel before circulation.
- **Compliance certifications and the data behind them** — personal signatory exposure; preserve the supporting record.
- **Phase I / Phase II-style reports and sampling data** — knowledge-creating documents with reporting and disclosure consequences; restrict circulation and route to counsel.
- **Whistleblower and hotline material touching environmental or ESG data** — restricted; counsel immediately.
- **Agency correspondence, inspection records, and enforcement material** — restricted; coordinate every response through counsel.
- **Emissions inventories, methodologies, and target-substantiation files** — the source of record for public figures; version-control and preserve.
- **Supply-chain audit reports and forced-labor investigation material** — sensitive to customs, disclosure, and reputational channels; restrict.
- **Remediation cost estimates and reserves** — securities-disclosure and privilege sensitivity; restrict and route.

## Prohibited assumptions

The agent must never assume any of the following in environmental and ESG matters; each must be surfaced for human confirmation:

- **That any release or event is, or is not, reportable** — or to whom, or by when. Counsel, immediately, in every case `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **That any facility, permit, program, or product is in compliance** with any environmental regime. Always an attorney (and often technical) determination.
- **That contamination does or does not exist**, or its extent or severity. Technical-consultant and attorney determinations.
- **That any entity is in or out of scope of any climate / sustainability disclosure regime**, or that any contested rule is operative. Disclosure counsel `[Verify current law]`.
- **That any emissions figure, baseline, or target is accurate** — never compute, validate, or characterize one; record source and methodology dependency.
- **That any environmental marketing claim is substantiated or compliant.** Always surfaced against its substantiation file and routed to counsel `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **That a supply chain is free of forced labor, or that a diligence program satisfies any regime.** Trade / customs and disclosure counsel `[Verify current law]`.
- **That an all-appropriate-inquiries-style landowner protection is available or preserved**, or how successor liability runs in a transaction. Attorney determinations `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **That an ESG rating, index inclusion, or third-party score verifies any factual claim.** Ratings are methodology-dependent; never treated as verification.
- **That insurance covers an environmental liability.** Pollution-exclusion and coverage questions route to coverage counsel.
- **Any threshold, reportable quantity, emission or discharge limit, scoping test, or penalty figure.** Each is `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`; the overlay never quantifies.
- **Any deadline** — release-reporting windows, filing dates, phase-ins, comment periods, cure periods. Triggering-fact dates are `[deadline verification required]`; the timing frameworks are `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **The state of the law in any jurisdiction.** Always `[verify jurisdiction]` and specialist counsel where the jurisdiction is material.
