# Environmental and ESG Overlay

This overlay tunes AgentCounsel's existing skills for **environmental and ESG** work — manufacturers and industrial operators, energy and chemicals companies, consumer-products companies making sustainability claims, public companies (and large private companies) within reach of climate- and sustainability-disclosure regimes, asset managers with ESG-labelled products, real-estate owners, developers, and lenders, importers with supply-chain diligence exposure, and the vendors and advisors that serve any of them. It is internal configuration material, not legal advice and not legal work product. A supervising attorney reviews it before use.

Environmental and ESG is a sector lens, not a body of law. The underlying legal work is regulatory work, securities-disclosure work, product-legal work, contracts work, and transactional-diligence work — coloured by permitting and compliance regimes, contaminated-property liability regimes, fast-moving climate- and sustainability-disclosure regimes, greenwashing exposure, and supply-chain diligence regimes. This overlay supplies that colouring; it never replaces the underlying skills, the `core/` operating rules, or the supervising attorney.

## 1. Scope of the overlay

### Skills this overlay tunes

The overlay is most often loaded alongside skills in these clusters:

## 2. The environmental and ESG regulatory architecture (orientation only — not legal advice)

This section orients an agent loading the overlay. None of it is a statement of law. Every framework reference carries `[Verify current law]` because this landscape spans federal, state, and local environmental regimes, securities regimes on active and contested rulemaking cycles, non-U.S. sustainability-reporting regimes with phased and amended application, and consumer-protection frameworks under revision — several of the disclosure regimes below have been adopted, stayed, revised, or withdrawn within single review cycles `[Verify current law]`.

### 2.1 Who is regulated, and for what

The first question in most environmental and ESG matters is *which regimes reach this entity, this facility, this product, or this statement?* This is fact-intensive and is an attorney determination — the overlay never concludes scope or applicability.

- **Operators of permitted facilities** — permitting regimes attach medium by medium (air, water, waste, chemicals) and facility by facility; the operative obligations live in the permit text, not only in the statute or rule `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **Owners and operators of contaminated or potentially contaminated property** — status-based liability regimes can attach through ownership or operation alone, past or present `[Verify current law]`.
- **Public companies and large private companies** — climate- and sustainability-disclosure regimes attach through listing status, size, or market presence, including extraterritorially `[Verify current law]`.
- **Importers and supply-chain participants** — forced-labor, conflict-minerals, and supply-chain due-diligence regimes attach through import activity, sector, or size `[Verify current law]`.
- **Makers of environmental marketing claims** — consumer-protection and advertising frameworks attach to the claim, whatever the industry.
- **Sponsors of ESG-labelled funds and investment products** — naming, labelling, and representation frameworks attach to the product and its documents `[Verify current law]`.

### 2.2 The principal regimes (framework level only)

Each regime has substantial substantive content beyond what is sketched here; the overlay surfaces the framework so a supervising attorney can apply the current text of the applicable rule. None of the following states a threshold, a quantity, a figure, or a deadline.

**Environmental permitting and compliance regimes (as categories)** — media-by-media frameworks governing air emissions, water discharges and stormwater, waste generation, storage, transport, and disposal, chemical registration and reporting, spill prevention, and species / habitat / project-review requirements `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`. Principal framework elements:
- Permits are facility-specific and condition-specific; the binding obligation is the permit's own text, limits, and monitoring conditions — always reviewed as the primary document.
- Self-monitoring, recordkeeping, and self-reporting frameworks are pervasive; compliance certifications frequently carry personal signatory exposure — surface every certification for counsel.
- Release- and spill-reporting frameworks impose notification obligations that can be immediate and can run to multiple agencies concurrently `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]` — timing is `[deadline verification required]` and the overlay never computes or concludes reportability (Section 4).
- Permit transfer, modification, and re-opener triggers attach to transactions, operational changes, and expansions — a recurring transactional gate.
- Several frameworks include citizen-suit-style private enforcement channels alongside agency enforcement `[Verify current law]`.

**Contaminated-property and remediation-liability regimes** — frameworks allocating cleanup liability for contaminated sites `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`. Principal framework elements:
- Status-based liability categories (current owner / operator, former owner / operator at the time of disposal, arranger, transporter styles); liability under some frameworks is described as strict, joint-and-several, and retroactive `[Verify current law]` — characterization in any matter is an attorney determination.
- Landowner protections keyed to pre-acquisition diligence — all-appropriate-inquiries-style standards implemented through Phase I-style environmental site assessments, with continuing-obligation elements after closing `[Verify current law]`. Whether a protection is available and preserved is an attorney determination in every case.
- State cleanup, brownfield, and voluntary-remediation programs as a category, with their own liability-protection instruments (no-further-action-style letters, covenants not to sue) `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- Institutional and engineering controls that restrict property use post-remediation and run with the land — a recurring diligence and drafting item.
- Vapor intrusion and emerging-contaminant categories are fast-moving `[Verify current law]` items that can reopen closed sites.
- Property-transfer disclosure obligations for known contamination exist in some jurisdictions `[verify jurisdiction]`.

**Climate and ESG disclosure regimes** — the fastest-moving layer in this overlay; every element below is a heavy `[Verify current law]` item whose status must be confirmed at the time of each review:
- **Securities-style climate-disclosure rules** — rules requiring climate-risk, governance, and (in some versions) emissions disclosure in securities filings have been adopted, stayed, litigated, revised, and withdrawn on active cycles; their operative status, scope, and content are `[Verify current law]` in every matter, and the overlay never assumes any version is in effect.
- **EU CSRD-style sustainability-reporting regimes** — double-materiality-style scoping, phased application by entity size and listing status, extraterritorial reach to non-EU groups, mandated reporting standards, and assurance requirements — each element has been amended on active legislative cycles and is `[Verify current law]`.
- **State climate-disclosure statutes as a category** — emissions-reporting and climate-risk-reporting statutes adopted at the state level, with their own scoping tests, phase-ins, and litigation posture `[verify jurisdiction]` `[Verify current law]`.
- **Voluntary-framework alignment** — investor-facing voluntary frameworks and targets create consistency exposure even where no mandate applies: a voluntary statement is still a statement, and inconsistency across documents is a recurring enforcement and litigation theory.
- **Emissions accounting** — greenhouse-gas inventories (scope-style categories) are methodological, assumption-laden technical work. The overlay never computes, verifies, or characterizes an emissions figure; every figure is a technical-verification and attorney-verification item.

**Greenwashing and environmental-marketing-claims frameworks** — consumer-protection and advertising frameworks reaching environmental claims `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`. Principal framework elements:
- Green-marketing guidance frameworks setting substantiation expectations for claims such as "recyclable," "compostable," "biodegradable," "carbon neutral," "net zero," and general environmental-benefit claims — under periodic revision `[Verify current law]`.
- EU-style green-claims and anti-greenwashing regimes as a category, with substantiation, verification, and comparative-claim elements `[Verify current law]`.
- Private and quasi-private challenge channels — competitor actions, consumer class actions, NGO complaints, and advertising-standards bodies — operating alongside regulators.
- The recurring exposure is mismatch: marketing claims vs. securities disclosures vs. sustainability reports vs. actual practice. Consistency review is the overlay's central greenwashing discipline.

**Supply-chain diligence regimes (as categories)** — frameworks imposing diligence, disclosure, or import consequences for supply-chain conduct `[Verify current law]`. Principal framework elements:
- Forced-labor import frameworks — import bans, detention mechanisms, and rebuttable-presumption structures keyed to regions or listed entities; enforcement runs through customs channels distinct from securities and consumer-protection channels `[Verify current law]`.
- Conflict-minerals disclosure frameworks — sourcing-inquiry and disclosure structures for specified minerals `[Verify current law]`.
- Supply-chain due-diligence and transparency statutes — from disclosure-statement-style regimes to EU-style substantive due-diligence duties with civil-liability elements; scope and content are on active legislative cycles `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- Contract flow-downs are the operational instrument — supplier codes, audit rights, traceability, and termination rights — and a recurring contracts-cluster item.

**ESG ratings, fund labelling, and investor-facing representations** — frameworks and exposure theories around ESG-labelled products and statements `[Verify current law]`. Principal framework elements:
- Fund-naming and labelling regimes as a category — name-driven investment-policy expectations and disclosure requirements `[Verify current law]`.
- ESG-integration representations in offering documents, RFP responses, stewardship reports, and marketing — the recurring enforcement theory is representation-versus-practice mismatch, not the merits of any ESG position.
- Third-party ESG ratings and index dependencies — ratings are methodology-dependent and divergent; the overlay never treats a rating as verification of any factual claim.
- Contrary-direction state laws and mandates exist concurrently in some jurisdictions, creating conflicting-obligation questions that route to counsel `[verify jurisdiction]`.

### 2.3 Jurisdiction and cross-border layers

Environmental and ESG obligations layer heavily. Each note below carries `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`:

- **Federal–state–local layering** — many environmental programs are administered by states under delegation, with states free to be stricter; local ordinances add a third layer. The operative rule for a facility is a jurisdiction-specific stack `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **Extraterritorial reach** — CSRD-style reporting and supply-chain due-diligence regimes reach corporate groups headquartered elsewhere through subsidiaries, turnover, or market presence `[Verify current law]`. Scope analysis is a specialist determination.
- **Facility portfolios** — each facility sits in its own permit, its own regulator relationship, and its own compliance history; portfolio-level statements aggregate facility-level facts and must be traceable to them.
- **Cross-border waste and product flows** — waste-shipment, product-stewardship, and extended-producer-responsibility frameworks as categories `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.

### 2.4 The transactional environmental layer

Deals touching facilities or real property carry a standard environmental-diligence architecture. The overlay surfaces the framework; scoping and interpretation are counsel and environmental-consultant work:

- **Phase I-style environmental site assessments** — the standard pre-acquisition diligence instrument, performed to a recognized standard, with a limited shelf life and defined user reliance `[Verify current law]`. The overlay never interprets a Phase I finding; it surfaces findings and gaps for counsel.
- **Phase II-style sampling decisions** — intrusive sampling creates knowledge, and knowledge can create reporting and disclosure duties. **The decision to sample routes to counsel before any sampling occurs** — a standing gate in every transaction.
- **Environmental representations and warranties** — compliance, permits, no-releases, no-proceedings, and disclosure-schedule interplay; knowledge and materiality qualifiers carry the negotiation weight.
- **Environmental indemnities, escrows, and insurance** — survival structures, caps and baskets, and pollution-legal-liability-style and reps-and-warranties insurance products with pollution exclusions to reconcile.
- **Permit transfers and change-of-control notices** — permits frequently require consent, notice, or re-issuance on a transaction; a missed transfer is a recurring post-closing failure.
- **Structure questions** — asset-versus-stock structuring and successor-liability doctrines materially change the environmental-liability allocation; always attorney determinations `[verify jurisdiction]`.

### 2.5 Terminology an agent should recognize and use precisely

These terms recur in environmental and ESG matters and carry specific meanings. The overlay does not redefine them; use them as the law and the industry use them, and confirm with counsel when the substantive meaning matters.

- **Phase I / Phase II ESA, all-appropriate-inquiries-style diligence, recognized environmental condition (REC)** — transactional-diligence terms of art.
- **Release, reportable quantity, spill reporting** — release-reporting terms; reportability is never concluded by the overlay `[Verify current law]`.
- **Permit, effluent / emission limit, monitoring report, compliance certification** — permitting terms; the permit text is the operative document.
- **NOV (notice of violation), consent decree / consent order, supplemental environmental project** — enforcement terms of art.
- **Potentially-responsible-party-style status categories (owner / operator, arranger, transporter)** — remediation-liability terms.
- **Brownfield, voluntary cleanup program, no-further-action-style letter, institutional / engineering controls** — remediation-program terms.
- **Vapor intrusion, emerging contaminants** — fast-moving contamination categories `[Verify current law]`.
- **Double materiality, sustainability statement, limited / reasonable assurance** — CSRD-style reporting terms `[Verify current law]`.
- **Scope-style emissions categories, GHG inventory, transition plan** — emissions-accounting terms; methodological, never computed by the overlay.
- **Net zero, carbon neutral, offsets, science-based targets** — claim vocabulary with substantiation weight; every use is a flagged claim.
- **Greenwashing, green-guides-style guidance, substantiation** — marketing-claims terms.
- **Forced-labor detention, withhold-release-style order, rebuttable presumption, conflict minerals** — supply-chain and customs terms `[Verify current law]`.
- **ESG fund labelling, names-rule-style requirements, stewardship report** — investment-product terms `[Verify current law]`.
- **Pollution legal liability (PLL) insurance, environmental indemnity, survival, baskets and caps** — transactional risk-allocation terms.
- **Citizen suit, notice letter** — private-enforcement terms `[Verify current law]`.

## 4. Escalation triggers specific to environmental and ESG

Conditions that, in this sector, must stop a workflow and route the matter to an environmental / ESG specialist or supervising attorney before proceeding:

- **Any release, spill, or contamination event — actual or suspected.** Route to counsel immediately. Reporting obligations can be immediate, can run to multiple agencies, and can attach personally `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`; timing is `[deadline verification required]`. The overlay never concludes whether an event is reportable and never computes a reporting window.
- **Any enforcement contact from any agency** — inspection, notice of violation, information request, subpoena, or civil investigative demand. Stop, preserve records, do not respond without counsel.
- **Discovery of possible unpermitted activity, permit exceedance, or a monitoring / reporting irregularity.** Counsel immediately; self-disclosure posture and audit-privilege questions are counsel work `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **Any proposal to conduct intrusive environmental sampling** (Phase II-style). Counsel before sampling — sampling creates knowledge, and knowledge can create duties.
- **A scoping question under any climate / sustainability disclosure regime** (securities-style, CSRD-style, state statutes). Disclosure counsel; the regimes' status and scope are `[Verify current law]` at the time of review.
- **A greenwashing demand letter, regulator inquiry, advertising-standards complaint, or competitor / NGO challenge.** Advertising and consumer-protection counsel.
- **A forced-labor detention, import hold, or customs inquiry.** Trade / customs counsel `[Verify current law]`.
- **A whistleblower allegation touching environmental compliance or ESG data.** Counsel immediately; preserve records.
- **A transaction touching contaminated or potentially contaminated property, a permitted facility, or a permit-transfer requirement.** Environmental transactional counsel.
- **An ESG-fund labelling, naming, or investor-representation question.** Investment-management / securities counsel `[Verify current law]`.
- **A citizen-suit notice letter.** Environmental-litigation counsel; the letter starts a framework-defined sequence `[Verify current law]` — dates are `[deadline verification required]`.
- **Any indication of knowing or willful conduct** in monitoring, reporting, or certification. Criminal exposure; specialist counsel immediately.
- **A conflicting-obligation question** — an ESG mandate in one jurisdiction against a contrary-direction law in another `[verify jurisdiction]`. Counsel resolves; the overlay surfaces both sides.

## 5. Standard environmental / ESG-counsel positions to surface (as questions, not conclusions)

These are recurring negotiation and documentation positions an environmental / ESG team needs to answer. The overlay surfaces the question; the organization (via `profile.md`) fills in the position; the supervising attorney confirms.

- **Facility and permit register.** Which facilities, which permits per facility, who owns renewal tracking, and who signs compliance certifications?
- **Release-response protocol.** Who is called first on a release, who determines reportability (counsel), who notifies which agencies, and who preserves records? Every timing element is `[deadline verification required]`.
- **Self-disclosure posture.** Under what circumstances does the organization consider voluntary self-disclosure, under which framework, and who decides `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- **Disclosure-regime register.** Which climate / sustainability disclosure regimes has counsel determined plausibly apply, what is each one's current status `[Verify current law]`, and who owns the scoping file?
- **Emissions-data governance.** What is the source of record for emissions figures, which methodology, who signs off, and how are figures kept consistent across documents?
- **Target-setting posture.** Who approves public environmental targets, what substantiation is required before publication, and what is the review cadence for aged targets?
- **Claims-substantiation program.** What substantiation file is required before any environmental marketing claim ships, who maintains it, and who clears "carbon neutral" / "net zero" / offset-based claims?
- **Sustainability-report governance.** Who drafts, who legal-reviews, and how is consistency with securities filings checked before publication?
- **Supply-chain diligence posture.** Which supplier code applies, what audit and traceability rights are standard, what triggers corrective action or termination, and who responds to customs inquiries?
- **Transactional environmental posture.** Standard Phase I-style requirements (age, standard, reliance), the sampling-decision protocol (counsel first), standard rep-and-indemnity positions, and the environmental-insurance posture.
- **Remediation-site management.** For each known site: program, consultant, counsel, reserve posture, and institutional-control obligations.
- **ESG-fund and investor-representation posture** (if applicable). Labelling standards used, representation-review process, and the consistency check between fund documents and practice `[Verify current law]`.
- **Conflicting-obligation protocol.** How the organization handles jurisdictions with contrary-direction ESG mandates `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **Document-retention posture for environmental records.** Monitoring data, certifications, assessments, and correspondence — retention schedules and litigation-hold interaction.

## 6. Loading sequence and interaction with other overlays

When this overlay is loaded, the agent should observe the load order described in `overlays/README.md`:

1. `core/` operating rules (the absolute floor).
2. The relevant practice profile from `practice-profiles/`.
3. The selected skill's `SKILL.md`.
4. This overlay's `OVERLAY.md` and `profile.md`.

This overlay frequently runs alongside other overlays where they exist — for example, the healthcare overlay for medical-waste and facility matters, or the fintech-payments overlay for ESG-labelled financial products. When multiple overlays apply, each adds gates and verification items; none subtracts. If two overlays appear to conflict on a position, the supervising attorney resolves; the agent surfaces the conflict and does not silently pick.

The overlay is also designed to compose with the cluster-level reference catalogs (`skills/contracts/references/red-flags.md`, `skills/antitrust-competition/references/risk-indicators.md`, `skills/securities-capital-markets/references/issue-spotting-frameworks.md`) — those catalogs handle the cross-sector patterns; this overlay adds the environmental- and ESG-layer attorney-verification items on top.

## 7. What this overlay does NOT do

- **No reportability determination.** The overlay never concludes that a release, spill, or event is or is not reportable, to whom, or by when. Counsel, immediately, in every case.
- **No compliance opinion.** The overlay never concludes that a facility, permit, program, or product is in compliance with any environmental regime `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **No contamination or condition conclusion.** Whether contamination exists, its extent, and its severity are technical-consultant and attorney determinations.
- **No disclosure-regime scoping conclusion.** The overlay never concludes that an entity is in or out of scope of any climate / sustainability disclosure regime, or that any version of a contested rule is operative `[Verify current law]`.
- **No materiality or disclosure-sufficiency conclusion.** Securities-disclosure sufficiency is disclosure-counsel work.
- **No emissions computation or verification.** Every emissions figure, baseline, and target is a technical-verification item; the overlay never calculates, validates, or characterizes one.
- **No claim-substantiation conclusion.** The overlay surfaces every environmental claim and its substantiation file; it never concludes a claim is substantiated or compliant.
- **No supply-chain clearance.** The overlay never concludes that a supply chain is free of forced labor or that a diligence program satisfies any regime `[Verify current law]`.
- **No landowner-protection or liability-allocation opinion.** Whether an all-appropriate-inquiries-style protection is available, or how successor liability runs, is an attorney determination.
- **No insurance-coverage opinion.** Pollution-exclusion and coverage questions route to coverage counsel.
- **No deadline computation.** Release-reporting windows, filing dates, phase-ins, comment periods, and cure periods are `[deadline verification required]` in every case.
- **No jurisdiction-of-the-week opinion.** Every jurisdiction-specific note carries `[verify jurisdiction]`; the overlay surfaces the question, never concludes.

## How to use this overlay

Load it, after the `core/` rules, the relevant practice profile, and the chosen skill, when the matter involves environmental or ESG facts. See `overlays/README.md` for the load order.

The overlay sharpens the skill for the sector. It adds gates, terminology, and attorney-verification items; it never replaces the skill, the `core/` rules, or the supervising attorney.
