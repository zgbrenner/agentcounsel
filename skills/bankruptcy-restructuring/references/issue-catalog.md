# Bankruptcy / Restructuring Issue Catalog

This catalog lists fact patterns a reviewer should scan for when organizing a
bankruptcy, restructuring, or distressed-transaction document or fact set for
an attorney. Each entry describes what to surface, why it matters, what to
record, and the questions it raises for the attorney to resolve.

Bankruptcy and restructuring skills organize facts and frame questions; they
do not conclude. Nothing here decides whether the automatic stay applies,
whether a claim is valid, allowed, secured, or entitled to priority, whether a
transfer is preferential or a defense applies, whether a contract is
executory or may be assumed, rejected, or assigned, whether a financing term
or lien priority is valid or sufficient, whether a plan is confirmable or
disclosure is adequate, or whether a sale may proceed free and clear. Every
entry surfaces a pattern and ends in questions for counsel — never a
conclusion, an approval, or a prediction of a ruling or a recovery.

This catalog is a review aid, not legal advice and not a legal conclusion.
Whether a surfaced pattern matters depends on the specific language, the
chapter and jurisdiction, the case posture, the facts supplied, and attorney
judgment. Every surfaced item is a candidate for attorney evaluation.

---

## How to Use This Catalog

For each pattern below:

1. Locate the provision, docket entry, or fact in the material under review —
   or note its absence, itself worth surfacing in many bankruptcy contexts.
2. Compare the pattern against the actual language or the facts as the user
   stated them. Quote language verbatim with its source citation; label each
   fact as a provided source, a user-stated fact, or `not provided` /
   `ambiguous`.
3. Record the pattern in the skill's issue-catalog scan, with the source
   citation and a neutral note. Never resolve an ambiguity; surface it.
4. Carry the pattern's **Questions for the attorney** into the deliverable as
   open questions for counsel. Do not answer them.
5. If any pattern in Section 1 (Automatic Stay & Post-Petition Action
   Patterns) is present, flag it prominently for immediate attorney attention
   before continuing — a potential post-petition action is always
   time-sensitive.

Mark every jurisdiction-dependent question `[verify jurisdiction]`, every
current-law question `[Verify current law]`, and **every date or period**
`[deadline verification required]` — a bar date, a challenge-period deadline,
a plan or disclosure-statement date, a hearing date, a cure-objection
deadline, a milestone, or any other bankruptcy deadline. Never invent a
statute, a dollar figure, a day count, a percentage, or a citation.

## 1. Automatic Stay & Post-Petition Action Patterns

The skill never concludes whether the stay applies, whether an exception
applies, or whether an action is permitted or a violation occurred. **Every
pattern here is a candidate for immediate escalation.**

### 1.1 Post-petition collection, enforcement, or communication activity

**Pattern:** A creditor, landlord, or counterparty takes or contemplates a
collection call, demand letter, litigation, setoff, foreclosure or
repossession step, lien enforcement, or contract termination after a stated
petition date.

**What to record:** The action, its timing relative to the stated petition
date (`[deadline verification required]`), and its source. Any notice sent
or received and any response given.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does the stay reach this action, and does any
exception apply `[verify jurisdiction]`? Should the action be stopped,
unwound, or addressed through a stay-relief motion or a violation claim?

---

### 1.2 Stay-relief motions and self-executing lift/modification language

**Pattern:** A cash-collateral order, DIP agreement, plan, or stipulation
modifies or lifts the stay automatically on a default or other trigger, or a
stay-relief motion is pending or filed.

**What to record:** The self-executing or stay-modification language
verbatim, its trigger, and the source. The status and filing date of any
stay-relief motion (`[deadline verification required]`).

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the self-executing modification
enforceable as drafted, and has it been triggered `[Verify current law]`?
Does a pending or resolved motion change what actions are permitted?

---

## 2. Claims & Proof-of-Claim Patterns

Claim facts are organized by component and classification assertion; the
skill never concludes validity, allowance, priority, or secured status, and
never computes an amount, interest, or a bar date.

### 2.1 Claim-component figures without a documented basis

**Pattern:** A claim states principal, interest, fees, or an adjustment with
a thin, inconsistent, or undocumented basis — a rate not tied to the
governing document, or fees claimed without a stated basis.

**What to record:** Each component, labeled user-stated or document-stated,
with its source. Any figure lacking a stated basis, and any two figures for
the same item that do not match, flagged `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is each component adequately documented and
consistent with the underlying agreement? Should post-petition interest be
treated as claimed under the governing standard `[verify jurisdiction]`?

---

### 2.2 Secured, priority, or perfection assertions without documentation

**Pattern:** A creditor asserts a security interest, lien, or priority
category (wages, taxes, deposits) without the underlying agreement, UCC
filing, or perfection documentation attached, or with it incomplete.

**What to record:** The asserted classification and basis; whether
perfection documentation is present, `not found`, or incomplete. Any
co-debtor, guaranty, or cross-collateralization arrangement as stated.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the asserted security interest valid and
perfected `[Verify current law]`? Does the asserted priority category apply
`[verify jurisdiction]`?

---

### 2.3 Duplicate, disputed, or insider-linked claims

**Pattern:** A claim appears to duplicate another on file, arises from the
same transaction as another claim, follows a disputed or non-payment
explanation already on record, or involves an insider or related-party
relationship.

**What to record:** Each dispute indicator found, with its source, or a note
that none surfaced. Any insider relationship as stated, uncharacterized.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does the dispute or duplication need
resolution before an objection or allowance decision? Does the insider
relationship raise a preference, fraudulent-transfer, or subordination
question?

---

### 2.4 Bar date and proof-of-claim preparation gaps

**Pattern:** A proof of claim is unfiled or its status is `unknown`, or
supporting documents (governing agreement, invoices, perfection documents,
signature authority) are incomplete.

**What to record:** Filing status and any user-supplied bar date
(`[deadline verification required]`). Missing supporting documents and any
redaction or signature-authority flag.

**Questions for the attorney:** Has the bar date been confirmed with the
claims agent, the court docket, or counsel? Are all supporting documents in
hand?

---

## 3. Preference & Avoidance-Action Defense Themes

Ordinary course, new value, and contemporaneous exchange are recorded
strictly as **facts to collect**, never as a defense conclusion; the skill
determines no preference liability and no available defense.

### 3.1 Ordinary-course-of-business facts

**Pattern:** The parties' course of dealing shows a pattern of payment
timing, amount, and method for the transfers at issue, or the transfers at
issue depart from that pattern.

**What to record:** The historical payment pattern from invoice and payment
history, with sources. Each disputed transfer's timing, amount, and method
alongside the pattern, flagging departures as facts.

**Questions for the attorney:** Do the transfers fit the ordinary course of
the parties' dealings or the industry `[Verify current law]`? What records
are missing to complete the comparison?

---

### 3.2 New-value facts

**Pattern:** The creditor extended new value — goods, services, or credit —
to the debtor after a transfer at issue, subsequently or contemporaneously.

**What to record:** Each instance of new value extended after a transfer,
with date, description, and amount as stated, and its source. Whether the
new value remains unpaid.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the new value adequately documented and
dated relative to each transfer? Does an unpaid balance affect the theme?

---

### 3.3 Contemporaneous-exchange facts

**Pattern:** A transfer is asserted to be a substantially contemporaneous
exchange for new value — for example, cash-on-delivery or check-for-goods.

**What to record:** The transfer date, the date value was exchanged, and the
gap, with sources. Any stated intent (invoice terms, contract language)
bearing on contemporaneity.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the gap short enough to support the theme
`[Verify current law]`? Is intent to exchange contemporaneously documented?

---

### 3.4 Security-interest and setoff facts bearing on avoidance

**Pattern:** A transfer involves a security interest granted, perfected, or
released during the look-back period, or a setoff the creditor asserts.

**What to record:** Each security interest and its perfection date as
stated, and any setoff amount, date, and asserted basis. Any unexplained gap
between the original and currently claimed balance.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does the timing of any lien grant or
perfection raise an avoidance question `[verify jurisdiction]`? Is the
asserted setoff supported by mutual, pre-petition obligations?

---

## 4. Executory Contract / Lease Assumption-Rejection Patterns

The skill never concludes whether a contract is executory, assumable,
rejectable, or assignable, and never determines a cure amount.

### 4.1 Cure amounts and default facts without independent confirmation

**Pattern:** A cure amount or default list is stated by one party without
independent support, or the debtor's and counterparty's stated figures do
not match.

**What to record:** The cure amount and each default as stated by its
source, with any cure-objection deadline (`[deadline verification
required]`). Any mismatch between stated figures, flagged `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the attorney:** What is the correct cure amount, and is it
supported by the contract and the parties' records? Are all defaults
identified?

---

### 4.2 Anti-assignment and consent-to-assignment language

**Pattern:** The contract or lease contains anti-assignment language, a
change-of-control trigger, or a consent requirement bearing on assumption and
assignment in a sale or plan context.

**What to record:** The anti-assignment or consent clause verbatim, with any
stated exceptions (affiliate transfers, permitted assigns). Any counterparty
position on assignment already asserted.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does the anti-assignment language survive or
is it overridden in this context `[Verify current law]`? Is counterparty
consent required, sought, or disputed?

---

### 4.3 Critical-vendor and business-impact assertions

**Pattern:** A party asserts a contract or vendor relationship is critical to
operations, or that rejection would cause a specific operational or customer
impact.

**What to record:** The critical-vendor assertion and impact as described,
with source, never confirmed. Any related deadline (`[deadline verification
required]`).

**Questions for the attorney:** Does the criticality assertion warrant
expedited treatment? What is the deadline by which assumption or rejection
must be decided?

---

## 5. DIP / Cash-Collateral Patterns

This section generalizes the taxonomy of terms a cash-collateral order or DIP
agreement commonly contains. It is a scan aid; deep line-by-line review
belongs in `cash-collateral-dip-financing-issue-spotter`. Approve no
financing term; determine no lien validity, perfection, or priority.

| Topic | Pattern to record | Frame for counsel |
|---|---|---|
| Collateral scope & priming | Collateral description (including unusual categories — avoidance-action proceeds, commercial tort claims), priming language, superpriority-claim provision, verbatim with sources. | What is the full collateral scope; which lienholders are primed or subordinated; is the priority acceptable? |
| Pre-/post-petition lien grants | Which liens secure pre-petition vs. post-petition obligations, and any cross-collateralization language, verbatim. | Does the document cross-collateralize, and what is the effect on the estate? |
| Roll-ups / creeping roll-ups | The structure — affected pre-petition debt, mechanics, timing — recorded as a structure, never characterized. | Is the roll-up appropriate, and what is its effect on creditor recoveries? |
| Budget & variance | Budget period, line items, variance percentages, testing periods, and amendment-approval mechanics, with budget-line references. | Are the budget lines adequate and thresholds workable? What follows a breach? |
| Adequate protection | The package (replacement liens, superpriority claims, cash payments, fee reimbursement, reporting) **as described** for each protected party. | What does each protected party receive, and is the package appropriate? |
| Carve-outs | What the carve-out covers, dollar caps, any post-trigger cap trigger, and exclusions, verbatim. | Does the carve-out reach all professionals and the wind-down amount? |
| Statutory-right waivers | Waivers in the pattern of surcharge or equities-of-the-case waivers, described generically; any cited statutory section quoted as the document's citation only. | What rights does the estate give up, and should the waiver be resisted? |
| Challenge / investigation period | Length and dates **exactly as stated** — never computed — marked `[deadline verification required]`; who may challenge; the budget for it. | Is the period and budget adequate, and who is bound absent a challenge? |
| Default triggers | Each default trigger and its notice/cure mechanic verbatim; every stay-interplay provision flagged and routed to `automatic-stay-issue-spotter/SKILL.md`. | Are the notice-and-cure mechanics adequate? What is the effect of a self-executing stay provision? |
| Use restrictions | Each permitted and prohibited use of cash collateral or DIP proceeds verbatim, with sources. | Do the restrictions impair the estate's ability to operate or investigate? |
| Releases / stipulations | Each release, waiver, admission, or stipulation; who gives it, who receives it, when binding. | What claims are released, who is bound, and is any release premature? |

## 6. Plan / Disclosure Statement Patterns

Plan and disclosure-statement facts are organized by class and cross-cutting
topic; the skill concludes nothing on confirmability, disclosure adequacy,
priority compliance, feasibility, or the voting outcome.

### 6.1 Classification and treatment consistency

**Pattern:** A class's stated classification and treatment appear in more
than one place with wording that does not plainly match, or similarly
situated claims appear classified differently without an explanation on the
face of the document.

**What to record:** The classification and treatment language for each
class, with sources; any internal mismatch flagged `ambiguous`. Similarly
situated claims placed in different classes, as described.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is each class's classification and treatment
internally consistent `[Verify current law]`? Does the scheme raise a
priority or equal-treatment question?

---

### 6.2 Releases, exculpation, and injunction provisions

**Pattern:** Third-party releases, an exculpation clause, or an injunction,
with a described scope of released parties and claims — sometimes including
non-debtor releases without an opt-out mechanism.

**What to record:** The released-party and released-claim definitions
verbatim; whether an opt-out/opt-in mechanism is described. The exculpation
clause's stated scope and standard.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the release and exculpation scope
permissible under the governing standard `[Verify current law]`? Is any
opt-out/opt-in mechanism adequate?

---

### 6.3 Feasibility facts and liquidation-analysis inputs

**Pattern:** Projections, a liquidation analysis, or feasibility assertions,
with underlying assumptions stated or unstated.

**What to record:** The stated figures with source and assumptions, marked
as provided, never verified. Any unstated assumption as a gap.

**Questions for the attorney:** Are the feasibility assumptions and
liquidation analysis adequately supported? Should a financial advisor test
the projections?

---

### 6.4 Voting and executory-contract/claims-reconciliation cross-references

**Pattern:** Voting procedures, assumed/rejected contract schedules, and
claims-reconciliation procedures cross-reference the claims register in ways
that may be inconsistent.

**What to record:** The voting procedure and eligibility as written; any
cross-reference that appears inconsistent. Every voting, objection, and
confirmation date (`[deadline verification required]`).

**Questions for the attorney:** Are voting eligibility and classification
consistent across the plan, disclosure statement, and claims register?

---

## 7. Distressed 363 Sale Patterns

Sale-process facts are organized by workstream; the skill concludes nothing
on whether assets may be sold free and clear or whether procedures are
sufficient.

### 7.1 Stalking-horse terms and bid-procedure asymmetries

**Pattern:** A stalking-horse agreement includes bid protections (break-up
fee, expense reimbursement, minimum overbid) alongside bid procedures setting
deadlines, qualification criteria, and an auction format.

**What to record:** The break-up fee, expense reimbursement, and
minimum-overbid terms; the bid deadline, qualification criteria, and auction
format (`[deadline verification required]`). Any term favoring the stalking
horse disproportionately.

**Questions for the attorney:** Are the bid protections and procedures likely
to be approved as designed `[Verify current law]`? Do the criteria and
deadlines allow genuine competing bids?

---

### 7.2 Liens, encumbrances, and cure costs on assigned contracts

**Pattern:** The sale contemplates a free-and-clear transfer and contract
assignment with cure costs, where the lien list or cure-cost schedule is
incomplete or contested.

**What to record:** Each lien or encumbrance and each assigned contract's
stated cure cost, with sources; any contract lacking a cure figure flagged
`not provided`. Any lienholder objection or cure dispute already raised.

**Questions for the attorney:** Are all liens and encumbrances identified
before the sale hearing? Are cure costs resolved or objected to, and by when
`[deadline verification required]`?

---

### 7.3 Closing deliverables and sale-order issues

**Pattern:** The sale checklist identifies closing deliverables (assignment
documents, releases, consents) and sale-order provisions (successor-liability
protection, injunction language) that remain open or contested.

**What to record:** Each closing deliverable, its responsible party, and
status. Any contested sale-order provision verbatim, with source.

**Questions for the attorney:** Are all closing deliverables on track, and
who owns each open item? Is the sale-order language adequate to protect the
buyer and the estate `[Verify current law]`?

---

## 8. Restructuring Term Sheet Patterns

Restructuring-document facts are organized role-aware; the skill concludes
nothing on enforceability or legal sufficiency.

### 8.1 Milestones, covenants, and default cross-references

**Pattern:** A restructuring support agreement or workout term sheet sets
milestones, financial or reporting covenants, and default triggers tied to
those milestones, sometimes cross-referencing a separate financing or plan
document.

**What to record:** Each milestone, its stated date, and any linked default
trigger, with sources, marking every date `[deadline verification
required]`. Any cross-reference to a DIP agreement, cash-collateral order, or
plan term sheet.

**Questions for the attorney:** What happens if a milestone is missed, and is
that consequence acceptable to the user's side? Are the milestones
achievable on the case timetable?

---

### 8.2 Voting and support obligations, and termination rights

**Pattern:** Parties commit to vote for or support a plan or transaction on
stated terms, with termination rights letting a party withdraw support on
defined events (milestone miss, competing proposal, material adverse
change).

**What to record:** The voting/support commitment and each termination
trigger verbatim, with sources. Any fiduciary-out or competing-proposal
carve-out to the support obligation.

**Questions for the attorney:** Are the termination rights symmetric between
the parties? Does a fiduciary-out undercut the support commitment?

---

### 8.3 Releases, fee provisions, and exclusivity

**Pattern:** Releases of claims among the restructuring parties, fee or
expense-reimbursement provisions for advisors, and an exclusivity or no-shop
covenant.

**What to record:** The release scope, fee/expense provisions, and
exclusivity covenant verbatim, with sources. Any carve-out to the release or
exclusivity.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the release scope acceptable and does it
survive termination? Do the fee provisions and exclusivity covenant match
market and the user's leverage?

---

## 9. Bar-Date / Deadline Criticality Cross-Cutting Flags

Deadlines recur across every bankruptcy skill; this section collects the
cross-cutting patterns to watch for regardless of which document is under
review. No deadline is ever computed here.

### 9.1 Multiple deadlines in tension across documents

**Pattern:** Two or more documents in the same matter (a bar-date notice, a
plan, a DIP order's milestones, a sale-procedures order) state dates that
appear to conflict or that cannot be reconciled without more information.

**What to record:** Every deadline found across the reviewed documents, with
source, marked `[deadline verification required]`. Any two dates that appear
to conflict or duplicate the same event, flagged `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the attorney:** Which date controls where documents appear
to conflict? Has each deadline been confirmed against the docket or the
claims agent?

---

### 9.2 Near-term or unconfirmed deadlines requiring escalation

**Pattern:** A deadline is unconfirmed (`unknown`, not yet calculated, or
dependent on a docket entry not in hand) or appears to fall soon relative to
the date of review.

**What to record:** Every unconfirmed or near-term deadline, its source (or
absence of one), and the responsible party, without estimating time
remaining. Whether the deadline depends on an event not yet occurred.

**Questions for the attorney:** Has this deadline been independently
confirmed with the court docket, the claims agent, or the case calendar? Is
escalation needed?

## Reviewer Notes

- A surfaced pattern is a prompt to look closely and to frame a question for
  the attorney — never a conclusion that the stay applies, a claim is valid
  or invalid, a transfer is avoidable, a contract is executory, a lien is
  valid or has priority, a plan is confirmable, or a sale may proceed free
  and clear.
- Nothing here computes a deadline, a claim amount, a cure amount, or an
  exposure figure. Those are all for the attorney.
- Section 1 patterns carry an immediate-escalation flag: on any automatic-
  stay or post-petition-action pattern, route it prominently for attorney
  attention before continuing the rest of the review.
- This catalog is not exhaustive. A document-specific or unusual pattern not
  listed here may still be worth surfacing under the nearest issue area,
  framed the same way — as facts to record and questions for counsel.
- See the individual `SKILL.md` files in `skills/bankruptcy-restructuring/`
  for the full review workflow each skill runs; this catalog supplies the
  pattern content that fills the tables defined in
  `skills/bankruptcy-restructuring/references/output-patterns.md`.
