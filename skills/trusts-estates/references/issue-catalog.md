# Trusts & Estates Issue Catalog

This catalog lists fact patterns a reviewer should scan for when organizing a
will, trust, estate-planning, probate, or estate-administration document or
fact set for an attorney. Each entry describes what to surface, why it is
worth surfacing, what to record, and the questions it raises for the
attorney to resolve.

Trusts and estates skills organize facts and frame questions; they do not
conclude. Nothing here assesses capacity, undue influence, fraud, or duress;
decides the validity or enforceability of an instrument; determines
beneficiary entitlement; finds a fiduciary breach or liability; states a
tax treatment, exemption, or exclusion; calculates a value, a deadline, or a
distribution; or predicts a court outcome. Every entry surfaces a pattern
and ends in questions for a qualified, licensed attorney — never a
conclusion.

This catalog is a review aid, not legal advice and not a legal conclusion.
Whether a surfaced pattern matters depends on the specific language, the
jurisdiction and its governing standard, the facts the parties supply, and
attorney judgment. Every surfaced item is a candidate for attorney
evaluation. Read this together with `core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md`,
whose safety-escalation and no-assessment rules govern the whole area and
control wherever they touch a pattern below.

---

## How to Use This Catalog

For each pattern below:

1. Locate the provision or fact in the material under review — or note its
   absence, which is itself worth surfacing in many trusts-and-estates
   contexts.
2. Compare the pattern against the actual language or the facts as the user
   stated them. Quote language verbatim with its article, section, or page
   reference; label each fact as a provided source, a user-stated fact, or an
   item that is `not provided` / `ambiguous`.
3. Record the pattern in the skill's issue-catalog scan, with the source
   reference and a neutral note. Never resolve an ambiguity; surface it.
4. Carry the pattern's **Questions for the attorney** into the deliverable as
   open questions for counsel. Do not answer them.
5. If any Section 9 pattern is present, stop expanding the deliverable and
   escalate immediately per `core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md` before
   continuing.

Mark every jurisdiction-dependent question `[verify jurisdiction]`, every
current-law question `[Verify current law]`, and every date or period
`[deadline verification required]`. Never invent a statute, a dollar figure,
an exemption amount, a percentage, or a citation. Mask account numbers and
full identifiers (SSN, taxpayer ID) by default wherever a fact touches one.

## 1. Testamentary & Trust Provision Issues

Dispositive and administrative provisions are organized as facts and plain-
language summaries — never characterized as fair, complete, or effective.

### 1.1 Dispositive provisions and residuary clause

**Pattern:** The instrument allocates specific bequests, a residuary estate,
and successor-taker provisions, and the residuary clause may be silent,
incomplete, or in apparent tension with the specific bequests.

**Why surface it:** A gap or tension in the residuary clause is a leading
source of later disputes over what happens to unallocated or lapsed
property; the skill records the language and does not resolve the tension.

**What to record:** Quote each dispositive and residuary provision with its
article or section reference, and note whether every category of property
appears to have a taker.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does the residuary clause cover every asset
not otherwise disposed of? Is there a tension between the specific bequests
and the residuary clause that needs resolution?

---

### 1.2 Powers of appointment and distribution standards

**Pattern:** The instrument grants a power of appointment (general or
limited) or sets a discretionary distribution standard (health, education,
maintenance, support, or an "ascertainable standard" formulation), with the
scope, permissible appointees, or standard left partly undefined.

**Why surface it:** The scope of a power and the clarity of a distribution
standard drive how a fiduciary may act and are frequent sources of
administration disputes; the skill records what is granted and does not
interpret its reach.

**What to record:** Quote the power-of-appointment or distribution-standard
language with its reference, and note which categories (health, education,
maintenance, support, other) are addressed or `not addressed`.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the scope of the power or standard clear
enough to administer without dispute? Does it match the settlor's or
testator's apparent intent as documented elsewhere?

---

### 1.3 Amendment, revocation, and restatement history

**Pattern:** The instrument has been amended, restated, or revoked one or
more times, and the version under review may not be the most recent, or the
chain of amendments is incomplete.

**Why surface it:** Acting on a superseded instrument is a serious and
avoidable error; the skill records the amendment history as provided and
never assumes which version currently governs.

**What to record:** List each version, amendment, or restatement with its
date (`[deadline verification required]`) and source, and flag any gap in
the chain as `not provided`.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the version under review confirmed to be
the current, controlling instrument? Are all amendments accounted for and
consistent with one another?

## 2. Execution & Formality Flags

Execution facts are recorded; validity is never assessed. Whether a
formality defect affects enforceability is a jurisdiction-specific legal
conclusion for the attorney alone.

### 2.1 Signature, witness, and notarization facts

**Pattern:** The signature block, witness signatures, dates, and
notarization or acknowledgment are complete, incomplete, inconsistent, or
absent.

**Why surface it:** Execution formalities vary by jurisdiction and
instrument type and can bear on validity; the skill records what is present
and what is missing without deciding whether execution was sufficient.

**What to record:** Note the presence or absence of each signature, witness
signature, date, and notarization, with page references, echoing every
execution date verbatim (`[deadline verification required]`).

**Questions for the attorney:** Do the execution formalities meet the
governing requirements for this instrument type `[Verify current law]`
`[verify jurisdiction]`? Are any required signatures or acknowledgments
missing?

---

### 2.2 Self-proving affidavit and attestation-clause facts

**Pattern:** The instrument includes, or omits, a self-proving affidavit or
an attestation clause reciting the witnesses' observations, and its
language may not track the jurisdiction's typical formulation.

**Why surface it:** A self-proving affidavit can simplify probate, and its
absence or a nonstandard attestation clause is worth flagging early; the
skill records what is present and reaches no conclusion on adequacy.

**What to record:** Quote the attestation clause and self-proving affidavit
(if present) with page references, and note if either is `not found`.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is a self-proving affidavit present, and if
not, what does probate require in its absence `[verify jurisdiction]`? Does
the attestation language meet current requirements `[Verify current law]`?

---

## 3. Fiduciary Duty & Conflict Issues

Fiduciary facts are organized for `fiduciary-duty-issue-spotter`; the skill
concludes no breach, liability, or compliance.

### 3.1 Self-dealing and conflict-of-interest facts

**Pattern:** A fiduciary transacts with the trust or estate, benefits
personally from a decision, or serves in a role (trustee, beneficiary,
family member, business partner) that creates a potential conflict.

**Why surface it:** Self-dealing and conflicted transactions are the core
subject of fiduciary-duty review; the skill records the facts neutrally and
never concludes that self-dealing occurred or that a conflict was improperly
resolved.

**What to record:** Record each transaction or role that presents a
potential conflict, with source, dates (`[deadline verification
required]`), and any disclosure, consent, or waiver on record.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does the transaction or role raise a
self-dealing or conflict-of-interest question `[verify jurisdiction]`? Was
appropriate disclosure or consent obtained and documented?

---

### 3.2 Investment and prudent-management facts

**Pattern:** The fiduciary's investment decisions, diversification (or lack
of it), and management of trust or estate assets are documented with
varying completeness, and a decision may depart from a stated investment
policy.

**Why surface it:** Prudent-investment questions turn on the facts of what
was decided and why; the skill records the decisions and any stated
rationale and does not evaluate prudence itself.

**What to record:** Record each investment decision or asset-management
action, its stated rationale (if any), and its source, noting any departure
from a written investment policy as `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the attorney:** Do the investment and management facts raise
a prudent-investor question `[Verify current law]`? Is the rationale for
any departure documented?

---

### 3.3 Accounting, distribution, and communication facts

**Pattern:** Required accountings, distribution decisions, and beneficiary
communications are complete, late, absent, or inconsistent with the
instrument's terms.

**Why surface it:** Gaps in accounting or communication are common sources
of beneficiary objections; the skill records what occurred and when, and
reaches no conclusion on adequacy or breach.

**What to record:** Record each accounting, distribution, and communication
event with its date (`[deadline verification required]`) and source, and
note any accounting or communication that appears `not provided` or overdue
as stated by the user.

**Questions for the attorney:** Were accountings and communications timely
and complete under the governing standard `[verify jurisdiction]`? Do any
distributions depart from the instrument's stated terms?

## 4. Beneficiary Designation & Titling Consistency

Beneficiary and titling facts are organized for
`beneficiary-designation-review`; the skill concludes nothing on legal
effect, which document controls, or beneficiary entitlement.

### 4.1 Beneficiary-designation versus estate-plan consistency

**Pattern:** A beneficiary designation on a retirement account, life
insurance policy, or payable-on-death account names someone other than, or
in different proportions than, the estate plan's dispositive scheme.

**Why surface it:** A designation controls the asset regardless of what the
will or trust says, and an inconsistency is one of the most common and
consequential estate-planning gaps; the skill flags the mismatch and does
not decide which document controls.

**What to record:** List each designation with the named beneficiaries and
percentages as stated, and compare against the estate plan's dispositive
scheme, flagging any mismatch `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does each designation reflect the client's
current intent? Which document controls disposition of each asset `[verify
jurisdiction]`?

---

### 4.2 Account and asset titling, and contingent-designation facts

**Pattern:** Account and asset titling (joint tenancy, tenancy in common,
payable-on-death, transfer-on-death, trust ownership) and contingent or
per-stirpes/per-capita designations are recorded with varying completeness
or apparent inconsistency across accounts.

**Why surface it:** Titling determines whether an asset passes by
survivorship, by designation, or through the estate, independent of the
will or trust; the skill records the titling as stated and does not
characterize its legal effect.

**What to record:** Record each account or asset's titling and any
contingent-designation language as stated, with source, and flag any
account whose titling appears inconsistent with the client's stated intent.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does the titling achieve the intended
disposition for each asset `[verify jurisdiction]`? Are contingent
designations complete and consistent across accounts?

---

## 5. Asset/Liability Inventory & Trust Funding Gaps

Inventory and funding facts are organized for
`asset-liability-inventory-builder` and `trust-funding-checklist`; the
skill appraises nothing and prepares no transfer document.

### 5.1 Inventory completeness and valuation-source facts

**Pattern:** The asset and liability inventory is missing a category
(real estate, business interests, digital assets, personal property,
debts), or a listed value's source and date are unclear.

**Why surface it:** An incomplete inventory undermines every downstream
step — tax analysis, funding, and distribution; the skill records what is
present and flags gaps without supplying or verifying a value.

**What to record:** List each asset and liability with its type, owner or
titling as stated, its user-supplied value and source (never computed), and
flag any category that appears `not provided`.

**Questions for the attorney:** Is the inventory complete across all asset
and liability categories? Which values need independent verification or
appraisal?

---

### 5.2 Trust-funding gaps

**Pattern:** An asset intended for a trust (per the instrument or the
client's stated intent) lacks funding evidence — a deed, assignment,
retitling, or beneficiary-designation change naming the trust.

**Why surface it:** An unfunded trust frequently fails to achieve its
intended purpose (probate avoidance, tax planning, incapacity protection);
the skill flags the gap and drafts no funding document.

**What to record:** List each asset intended for the trust, whether funding
evidence is present or `not found`, and the responsible party for
completing funding, with source.

**Questions for the attorney:** Which assets remain unfunded, and what
steps are needed to complete funding? Does an unfunded asset defeat a
planning goal the instrument states?

---

### 5.3 Business-interest and closely held asset facts

**Pattern:** The inventory includes a business interest, closely held
entity, or illiquid asset with unclear valuation basis, buy-sell
provisions, or succession terms.

**Why surface it:** Closely held and illiquid assets raise valuation,
liquidity, and succession questions distinct from marketable assets; the
skill records the facts and does not value the interest or interpret a
buy-sell agreement's effect.

**What to record:** Record each business or closely held interest, its
ownership percentage as stated, any buy-sell or succession document
referenced (or `not found`), and the stated valuation basis, with source.

**Questions for the attorney:** Does a buy-sell or succession document
govern this interest, and is it consistent with the estate plan? Is
independent valuation needed before further planning or administration?

## 6. Estate/Gift/GST Tax Issue Categories

Facts organized for `estate-tax-issue-intake`; the skill calculates no tax,
exemption, exclusion, or threshold, and reaches no tax-treatment conclusion.

| Tax area | Facts to record | Question for the tax professional |
|---|---|---|
| Estate-tax inclusion | Retained interests, powers, or incidents of ownership the decedent held over an asset, as stated | Does this asset raise an estate-tax inclusion question `[Verify current law]`? |
| Marital and charitable transfers | Marital-deduction or charitable-bequest language, and any condition attached, verbatim | Does the transfer qualify for the deduction claimed `[Verify current law]`? |
| Gift-tax and lifetime-transfer facts | Lifetime gifts, their stated dates (`[deadline verification required]`) and recipients, and any gift-tax return referenced | Are all reportable lifetime transfers identified and consistently documented? |
| GST tax and skip-person facts | Transfers to a beneficiary two or more generations below the transferor, and any GST exemption allocation referenced | Does a transfer involve a skip person, and is exemption allocation documented `[Verify current law]`? |
| Foreign-asset or foreign-person facts | Foreign real property, accounts, or a non-citizen spouse or beneficiary, as stated | Does foreign ownership or a non-citizen party change the tax treatment `[verify jurisdiction]`? |

Cite every figure in every row to its source document or state `not
provided`; never total, verify, or apply an exemption or exclusion amount.

## 7. Probate & Post-Death Administration Task Categories

Facts organized for `probate-document-checklist` and
`post-death-administration-task-tracker`; no filing-ready form is prepared
and no deadline is calculated.

| Task category | Facts to record | Question for the attorney |
|---|---|---|
| Probate document readiness | Death certificate, original will/codicils, petition documents, and their status (collected/partial/outstanding) | Are all documents required to open probate on hand `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Creditor claims and notice | Creditor notices given or required, claims received, and their stated deadlines (`[deadline verification required]`) | Has required creditor notice been given, and are claim deadlines confirmed against the court or statute `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Fiduciary appointment | Petition for appointment, bond requirements, and letters testamentary/administration status | Is the fiduciary properly appointed, and is a bond required or waived `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Administration task sequencing | Asset marshaling, tax filings, accountings, and distribution tasks, with responsible party and status | Are administration tasks sequenced correctly, and which are blocked on another task? |

Cite every item to its source document or state `not provided`; flag every
near-term or unconfirmed deadline for immediate attorney attention.

## 8. Estate Litigation & Contest Fact Patterns

Facts organized for `estate-litigation-facts-chronology`; the skill assesses
no merits and predicts no outcome.

### 8.1 Will or trust contest ground facts

**Pattern:** The facts touch on a ground a contest is commonly built on —
lack of capacity, undue influence, fraud, duress, improper execution, or a
later, inconsistent instrument — without the skill assessing whether any
ground is established.

**Why surface it:** These are the facts an attorney needs organized to
evaluate a potential contest; recording them neutrally, with sources, is
the whole of the skill's role. Route any Section 9 indicator per the
escalation rule before continuing.

**What to record:** Record each fact bearing on a contest ground, with its
source and date (`[deadline verification required]`), labeled
disputed/undisputed as the user provides it — never resolved by the skill.

**Questions for the attorney:** Do these facts, taken together, warrant
further investigation of a contest? What additional records would clarify a
disputed fact?

---

### 8.2 Breach-of-fiduciary-duty claim facts

**Pattern:** A beneficiary or co-fiduciary asserts a fiduciary breach —
tied to the self-dealing, investment, or accounting facts organized in
Section 3 — with the underlying facts disputed or only partly documented.

**Why surface it:** A breach assertion needs its factual basis organized
before an attorney can evaluate it; the skill never concludes a breach
occurred or that liability follows.

**What to record:** Record the assertion, the facts offered in support, and
the facts offered in response, each with source and disputed/undisputed
status as provided.

**Questions for the attorney:** Are the competing factual accounts
sufficiently documented to evaluate the claim? What discovery or additional
records would resolve the disputed facts?

---

Where a chronology assembled from multiple sources contains two or more
accounts of the same event that do not match, record every account side by
side with its source and mark the event `disputed` — never resolve which
account controls.

## 9. Capacity, Undue Influence & Vulnerable-Party Indicators

These patterns govern the whole catalog. If any is present, stop expanding
the deliverable, surface the concern prominently at the top of the output
flagged for the supervising attorney, and escalate per
`core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md`. Include any user-provided
emergency, adult-protective-services, or hotline resources; never invent
referral resources. These facts are recorded neutrally and never
characterized: the skill never assesses capacity, undue influence, fraud,
or duress, and never concludes that any occurred.

### 9.1 Capacity indicators

**Pattern:** The facts reference a medical diagnosis, cognitive decline,
hospitalization, guardianship or conservatorship proceeding, or a
significant change in the principal's condition at or near the time of a
document's execution.

**Why surface it:** Capacity at execution is central to validity and is a
legal and sometimes medical judgment; the skill records only what a
provided document or the user states, and never characterizes a medical or
psychological condition beyond that.

**What to record:** Record each capacity-related fact as stated, with
source and date (`[deadline verification required]`), and note its
proximity to the document's execution date.

**Questions for the attorney:** Do these facts raise a capacity question
requiring further evaluation `[Verify current law]`? What records
(medical, witness, attorney's contemporaneous notes) would inform that
evaluation?

---

### 9.2 Undue-influence, isolation, and coercion indicators

**Pattern:** The facts show isolation of the principal from family, a
caregiver or new acquaintance newly involved in financial or legal
decisions, a sudden or unexplained change favoring a new beneficiary, or
pressure or urgency surrounding execution.

**Why surface it:** These are facts for attorney evaluation, recorded
neutrally; the skill never labels conduct as undue influence or coercion
and never concludes that any occurred.

**What to record:** Record each indicator as a fact, with its source and no
characterization, noting the timing relative to any change in the estate
plan (`[deadline verification required]`).

**Questions for the attorney:** Do these facts raise an undue-influence
question for the attorney to evaluate? What independent records would
clarify the sequence of events?

---

### 9.3 Elder abuse and financial-exploitation indicators

**Pattern:** The facts include indicators of possible elder abuse or
financial exploitation — unexplained account withdrawals, a new
power-of-attorney holder making unusual transactions, missing funds, or a
caregiver's financial control over the principal.

**Why surface it:** A safety and financial-exploitation concern escalates
immediately and is never framed as an assessment of whether abuse occurred;
the skill records what is reported and routes it.

**What to record:** Record each indicator as a fact, with source and date
(`[deadline verification required]`), and note any report already made to
adult protective services, law enforcement, or a financial institution.

**Questions for the attorney:** Has this concern been escalated per
`core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md` before other work continued? Does
a report need to be made to adult protective services or another agency
`[verify jurisdiction]`?

## Reviewer Notes

- A surfaced pattern is a prompt to look closely and to frame a question for
  the attorney — never a conclusion that an instrument is valid, a fiduciary
  breached a duty, a beneficiary is entitled, a designation controls, or a
  tax treatment applies.
- Nothing here assesses capacity, undue influence, fraud, or duress; decides
  validity or enforceability; determines beneficiary entitlement; finds a
  fiduciary breach; states a tax treatment; or computes a value, an
  exemption, or a deadline. Those are all for the attorney.
- Section 9 patterns override the ordinary workflow: on any capacity,
  undue-influence, or vulnerable-party indicator, escalate immediately per
  `core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md` before continuing, and route to
  the appropriate resource, including adult protective services where the
  facts warrant it.
- This catalog is not exhaustive. A document-specific or unusual pattern not
  listed here may still be worth surfacing under the nearest issue area,
  framed the same way — as facts to record and questions for counsel.
- See the individual `SKILL.md` files in `skills/trusts-estates/` for the
  full review workflow each skill runs; this catalog supplies the pattern
  content that fills the tables defined in
  `skills/trusts-estates/references/output-patterns.md`.
