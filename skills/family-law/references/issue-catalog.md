# Family Law Issue Catalog

This catalog lists fact patterns and provision types a reviewer should scan for when organizing a family-law document or fact set for an attorney. Each entry describes what to surface, why it is worth surfacing, what to record, and the questions it raises for the attorney to resolve.

Family-law skills organize facts and frame questions; they do not conclude. Nothing here scores a parent, spouse, or child; assesses capacity, undue influence, coercion, or abuse; decides whether property is separate, marital, or community; values a claim; computes support or a deadline; or predicts how a court will rule. Every entry surfaces a pattern and ends in questions for a qualified, licensed attorney — never a conclusion, a fairness judgment, a best-interests assessment, or a statement that a provision favors one party.

This catalog is a review aid, not legal advice and not a legal conclusion. Whether a surfaced pattern matters depends on the specific language, the jurisdiction and its governing standard, the facts the parties supply, and attorney judgment. Every surfaced item is a candidate for attorney evaluation. Read this together with `core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md`, whose safety-escalation and no-assessment rules govern the whole area and control wherever they touch a pattern below.

---

## How to Use This Catalog

For each pattern below:

1. Locate the provision or fact in the material under review — or note its absence, which is itself worth surfacing in many family-law contexts.
2. Compare the pattern against the actual language or the facts as the user stated them. Quote language verbatim with its section reference; label each fact as a provided source, a user-stated fact, or an item that is `not provided` / `ambiguous`.
3. Record the pattern in the skill's issue-catalog scan, with the section reference or source and a neutral note. Never resolve an ambiguity; surface it.
4. Carry the pattern's **Questions for the attorney** into the deliverable as open questions for counsel. Do not answer them.
5. If any Section 5 pattern is present, stop expanding the deliverable and escalate immediately per `core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md` before continuing.

Mark every jurisdiction-dependent question `[verify jurisdiction]`, every current-law question `[Verify current law]`, and every date or period `[deadline verification required]`. Never invent a statute, a dollar figure, a day count, a percentage, or a citation.

## 1. Custody and Parenting Provisions

Custody and parenting terms are organized as factors under the jurisdiction's governing standard — never scored, ranked, or measured against a best-interests conclusion. The weighing is the attorney's.

### 1.1 Legal custody and decision-making allocation

**Pattern:** The document allocates decision-making authority — education, medical, religious, extracurricular — jointly, solely, or by subject area, and may or may not say how a disagreement is broken.

**Why surface it:** How authority is allocated and whether a tie-breaker exists drives day-to-day administration; silence on tie-breaking is a common source of later friction.

**What to record:**
- Quote the decision-making language with its section reference, and note which categories are addressed and which are `not addressed`.
- Note any tie-breaker, mediation-first, or final-authority mechanism, or its absence.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Does the allocation match the parties' intent and the governing standard `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- Is any category left unassigned, and how would a deadlock be resolved?

---

### 1.2 Physical custody and the residential schedule

**Pattern:** The residential schedule is stated as a label ("joint," "primary," a percentage) without a concrete day-by-day breakdown, or the schedule text and the label or percentage do not plainly line up.

**Why surface it:** A label or percentage not backed by a specific, workable schedule can be administered in more than one way; the skill records the schedule as written and never computes a parenting-time percentage.

**What to record:**
- Quote the schedule language and any labels or percentages, and note whether a concrete weekly, holiday, and summer schedule is specified or only summarized.
- Flag any mismatch between a stated label or percentage and the schedule text as `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Does the written schedule support the label or percentage used elsewhere?
- Are exchange times, places, and responsibilities specified enough to administer without dispute?

---

### 1.3 Right of first refusal

**Pattern:** A right-of-first-refusal clause requires a parent to offer parenting time before using third-party care, but the trigger, notice method, and response window are vague or unstated.

**Why surface it:** A right of first refusal that does not define its own trigger and mechanics is a recurring enforcement-friction point; the skill surfaces the gap without supplying a threshold.

**What to record:**
- Quote the clause and note the stated trigger duration (echoed as written, `[deadline verification required]`) or mark it `not addressed`.
- Note the notice and response mechanics, or their absence.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Is the trigger threshold defined clearly enough to enforce?
- Are notice and response mechanics specified and workable for these parties?

---

### 1.4 Relocation and travel-notice provisions

**Pattern:** The document addresses relocation of a child, out-of-area or out-of-jurisdiction travel, or passport and travel-consent handling — or is silent on one or more of these.

**Why surface it:** Relocation and travel provisions carry jurisdiction-specific notice and consent requirements, and their absence is a common gap.

**What to record:**
- Quote any relocation, travel-notice, or travel-consent language, and echo any notice period or distance threshold verbatim, marked `[deadline verification required]`.
- Note which of relocation, travel notice, and travel consent are `not addressed`.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Do the relocation and notice provisions meet the governing requirements `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- Are travel-consent and passport handling addressed, and are the notice periods verifiable?

## 2. Child Support Factors

Child support inputs are organized, never computed. The skill records the facts a guideline would take as inputs; it never runs a worksheet, applies a formula, imputes income, or states an amount or arrears figure.

### 2.1 Income sources and their documentation

**Pattern:** The material states income from wages, self-employment, bonuses, commissions, overtime, benefits, or other sources — with varying documentation and sometimes conflicting figures.

**Why surface it:** Guideline calculation depends on the income inputs, and undocumented or inconsistent figures are the most common reason a support review stalls.

**What to record:**
- Record each income figure as user-stated or drawn from a specific document, with the source, and list which sources are undocumented.
- Flag any two figures for the same item that do not match as `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Which income figures are documented, and which need supporting records before a guideline is applied?
- Are self-employment, variable, or in-kind income sources captured completely?

---

### 2.2 Imputation and earning-capacity questions

**Pattern:** The facts raise a question about earning capacity — unemployment, underemployment, a recent income change, or a gap between lifestyle and reported income — that a court could treat as an imputation question.

**Why surface it:** Whether income should be imputed is a legal determination; the skill records the underlying facts neutrally and never concludes that a party is voluntarily underemployed or that income should be imputed.

**What to record:**
- Record the employment and income-history facts as stated, with sources and no characterization, echoing related dates `[deadline verification required]`.
- Mark unexplained gaps as facts to evaluate, not as findings.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Do these facts raise an imputation or earning-capacity question under the governing standard `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- What records would counsel need to evaluate earning capacity?

---

### 2.3 Add-on expenses and deviation factors

**Pattern:** The material references add-on expenses — childcare, health insurance, uninsured medical, education, extraordinary needs — or a support figure that departs from a guideline result, with or without a stated reason.

**Why surface it:** Add-ons and any deviation from a guideline are inputs and factors the attorney evaluates, not amounts the skill computes; a stated deviation with no recorded rationale is worth flagging.

**What to record:**
- List each add-on expense as stated, with its source and how it is allocated (or `not addressed`).
- Record any stated deviation from a guideline result and quote any rationale, without assessing it; echo figures verbatim.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Are all add-on categories the guideline recognizes accounted for `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- If support deviates from the guideline, is the basis documented and supportable?

---

## 3. Spousal Support and Maintenance Factors

Spousal support or maintenance factors are organized as inputs. The skill never calculates an amount or duration, weighs the statutory factors, determines entitlement, or imputes earning capacity.

### 3.1 Marriage-length and duration facts

**Pattern:** The material states marriage and separation dates, relationship duration, or a support-duration term, sometimes inconsistently across documents.

**Why surface it:** Duration facts are inputs to the maintenance analysis and to any durational limit the jurisdiction applies; the skill echoes the dates for verification and applies no durational rule.

**What to record:**
- Echo marriage, separation, and any support-term dates verbatim, marked `[deadline verification required]`, and flag any inconsistency across documents as `ambiguous`.
- Record any stated support duration as written, without validating it.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Are the marriage and separation dates confirmed, and do they trigger any durational rule `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- Does the stated support duration correspond to the facts and the governing standard?

---

### 3.2 Amount, modifiability, and termination triggers

**Pattern:** A support amount is stated — possibly with step-downs, a cost-of-living adjustment, or express modifiable / non-modifiable language — and it terminates or is reviewed on events such as remarriage, cohabitation, death, retirement, or a fixed date, sometimes only partly defined.

**Why surface it:** Whether and how support can change or end drives administration and whether it can be revisited; non-modifiability language and undefined triggers like "cohabitation" are frequent sources of later dispute.

**What to record:**
- Quote the amount, any step-down or COLA language, any modifiability / non-modifiability language, and each termination or review trigger, with section references.
- Note whether each trigger is defined (for example, how "cohabitation" is measured); echo every figure and date verbatim and calculate nothing.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Are the modifiability terms enforceable and intended `[Verify current law]`?
- Is each triggering event defined precisely enough to apply, and how would it be proved?

---

## 4. Property and Debt Division

Property and debt patterns are organized as characterization, valuation, and disclosure questions. The skill never characterizes an item as separate, marital, or community; never values or appraises; and never computes equity, net worth, or a division.

### 4.1 Characterization questions — separate vs. marital or community

**Pattern:** The material treats an asset or debt as separate or as marital/community property, or the facts — pre-marital origin, inheritance, gift, commingling, post-separation acquisition — raise a characterization question.

**Why surface it:** Characterization turns on jurisdiction-specific rules and is a legal determination; the skill records the facts and the label the document uses and reaches no characterization itself.

**What to record:**
- Record each item, the label the document assigns (if any), and the facts bearing on its origin, with sources; mark the characterization itself a `[verify jurisdiction]` question — never state it.
- Flag commingling, tracing, or post-separation-acquisition facts as items to evaluate.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- How is each item characterized under the governing rules `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- Do any items require tracing, and are the records to do so available?

---

### 4.2 Valuation-date and valuation-method questions

**Pattern:** Values are stated for assets — real estate, businesses, investment or retirement accounts — with the valuation date and method sometimes unstated, inconsistent, or drawn from different points in time.

**Why surface it:** The date and method of valuation can change a number materially and are contested inputs; the skill records stated values with their source and date and never appraises or reconciles them.

**What to record:**
- Record each stated value with its source, its stated valuation date (`[deadline verification required]`), and its method if given, noting where a date or method is `not provided`.
- Flag conflicting values for the same asset as `ambiguous`, without choosing between them.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- What valuation date and method govern each asset `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- Which assets need a professional appraisal or updated statement?

---

### 4.3 Retirement accounts and QDRO flags

**Pattern:** The material references a pension, 401(k), IRA, or other retirement or deferred-compensation interest to be divided, with or without reference to a qualified domestic relations order or equivalent division mechanism.

**Why surface it:** Dividing a retirement interest usually requires a separate order and plan-specific mechanics, and a division that names no vehicle is a common gap; the skill surfaces the flag and drafts no order.

**What to record:**
- List each retirement or deferred-comp interest referenced, with its source, and note whether a QDRO or equivalent division mechanism is referenced or `not addressed`.
- Record any stated division terms verbatim, without computing shares.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Does each divided retirement interest have an appropriate division order and plan-compliant mechanics `[Verify current law]`?
- Are survivor-benefit, loan, gain/loss, and tax-treatment questions addressed?

---

### 4.4 Hidden-asset and dissipation indicators

**Pattern:** The facts show indicators an attorney may wish to evaluate — undisclosed or unexplained accounts, transfers to third parties, an unexplained drop in a balance, income inconsistent with lifestyle, or missing statements.

**Why surface it:** These are facts for attorney evaluation, not findings; the skill records them neutrally and never concludes that assets were hidden or dissipated, and never advises any party to hide, move, or undervalue anything.

**What to record:**
- Record each indicator as a fact, with its source and no characterization, noting missing periods, missing accounts, or unexplained transfers as gaps to evaluate.
- Echo any related dates and figures verbatim; draw no inference.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Do these facts warrant further discovery or a forensic review?
- What records are missing that would resolve the open questions?

## 5. Safety and Vulnerable-Party Indicators

These patterns govern the whole review. If any is present, stop expanding the deliverable, surface the concern prominently at the top of the output flagged for the supervising attorney, and route to `domestic-violence-safety-referral-checklist`, per `core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md`. Include any user-provided emergency or hotline resources; never invent referral resources. These facts are recorded neutrally and never characterized: the skill never assesses capacity, undue influence, coercion, or abuse, and never concludes that any occurred.

### 5.1 Domestic-violence and protective-order indicators

**Pattern:** The material references domestic violence, a protective or restraining order, described incidents, or safety-driven provisions such as supervised exchanges or no-contact carve-outs.

**Why surface it:** A safety dimension changes how the matter is handled and must be escalated immediately, ahead of other organizing work; the skill records what the user reports without pressing for detail or weighing it.

**What to record:**
- Record any protective or restraining order by type and date as stated (`[deadline verification required]`), and any safety-driven provision (supervised exchange, no-contact, protected address) and its source.
- Record described incidents only as stated, neutrally, with no added detail and no assessment of whether they occurred.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Has the safety concern been escalated and routed to `domestic-violence-safety-referral-checklist`?
- Do existing orders and safety provisions need to be verified and reconciled with the rest of the document?

---

### 5.2 Child-safety indicators

**Pattern:** The facts raise a child-safety concern — allegations of abuse or neglect, a safety-driven supervised-visitation term, a child-protection agency involvement, or similar.

**Why surface it:** Facts about children are organized only as matter facts for the attorney and carry heightened sensitivity; a child-safety concern escalates immediately and is never framed as advice to, or an assessment of, a child.

**What to record:**
- Record the child-safety facts only as stated, neutrally, with sources and no characterization, including any supervised-visitation, safe-exchange, or agency-involvement term.
- Treat all child identifiers as high-sensitivity and mask them to what the review requires.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Has this concern been escalated per `core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md` before other work continued?
- Do any child-protection or agency proceedings need to be identified and reconciled with the document?

---

### 5.3 Coercion, pressure, and impairment indicators

**Pattern:** The facts include indicators an attorney may wish to evaluate — pressure or urgency to sign, one party's control over the other's information or finances, isolation or dependency, or references to substance abuse or a mental-health condition.

**Why surface it:** These are facts requiring attorney evaluation, recorded neutrally; the skill never labels conduct as coercion, undue influence, or abuse, never assesses capacity, and never characterizes a medical or psychological condition beyond what a provided document states.

**What to record:**
- Record each indicator as a fact, with its source and no conclusion, recording no more than a provided document states about any medical or psychological reference.
- Flag any signs that a party may be unrepresented for the attorney's attention.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Do these facts raise a capacity, undue-influence, or voluntariness question for the attorney to evaluate?
- Is either party unrepresented, and does that change how the matter should proceed?

## 6. Enforceability and Formality Flags

Enforceability and formality turn on current, jurisdiction-specific law. The skill flags the formal features and gaps and reaches no enforceability or validity conclusion; mark each such question `[Verify current law]`.

### 6.1 Execution and signature formalities

**Pattern:** The execution block, notarization or acknowledgment, dates, initials on key pages, or witnessing is incomplete, inconsistent, or absent, or the document appears unsigned or partly signed.

**Why surface it:** Execution formalities can bear on validity and vary by jurisdiction and document type; the skill records what is present and what is missing and does not decide whether the execution is sufficient.

**What to record:**
- Note the presence or absence of each signature, date, initial, notarization, and witness, with references, echoing every execution date verbatim (`[deadline verification required]`).
- Flag any inconsistency between the execution block and the body of the document.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Do the execution formalities meet the governing requirements for this document type `[Verify current law]`?
- Are any required signatures, dates, or acknowledgments missing?

---

### 6.2 Financial-disclosure and affidavit completeness

**Pattern:** The agreement recites that financial disclosures or affidavits were exchanged, or waives them, and the referenced disclosures may be absent, incomplete, or inconsistent with the schedules.

**Why surface it:** Many jurisdictions condition enforceability of a marital agreement on adequate financial disclosure; the skill surfaces whether the recited disclosures are present and consistent and does not judge their adequacy.

**What to record:**
- Quote any disclosure recital or waiver with its section reference, and note whether the referenced disclosures or affidavits are attached, `not found`, or inconsistent with the asset/debt schedule.
- Record any waiver of disclosure as written, without assessing it.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Was disclosure adequate for enforceability under the governing standard `[Verify current law]`?
- If disclosure was waived, is the waiver valid and knowing under that standard?

---

### 6.3 Restricted waivers and independent-counsel status

**Pattern:** The document waives rights some jurisdictions restrict or condition — future support, child-support rights that may be non-waivable, a statutory share, counsel — or recites the parties' representation status.

**Why surface it:** Certain waivers are limited, conditioned, or unenforceable depending on current law, and representation status can bear on enforceability; the skill flags the waivers and the recital and concludes on neither.

**What to record:**
- List each waiver of a right, quote its language and section reference, and flag any waiver that appears to reach a right that may be restricted.
- Record any recital of independent counsel, or of a party proceeding without counsel, as written.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Is each waiver permitted, conditioned, or restricted under current law `[Verify current law]` `[verify jurisdiction]`?
- Does the representation status affect enforceability, and is any required counsel recital present?

---

## 7. Tax and Benefits Hooks

Tax and benefits questions are surfaced and routed to tax counsel or a benefits specialist. The skill never determines a tax consequence, an eligibility outcome, or a filing position.

### 7.1 Dependency and child-related tax items

**Pattern:** The document allocates the child dependency exemption or credit, head-of-household treatment, or a related child tax item between the parents, or is silent on them.

**Why surface it:** Allocation of child-related tax items is a common term with consequences a tax professional evaluates; the skill records the allocation and flags the routing and computes nothing.

**What to record:**
- Quote any allocation of dependency, credit, or filing-status items with section references, and note which child-related tax items are `not addressed`.
- Note any required form or election referenced, without describing its effect.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Should the dependency and child-tax allocation be reviewed with tax counsel?
- Are the mechanics (forms, elections, conditions) complete and consistent?

---

### 7.2 Support and transfer characterization, and benefits hooks

**Pattern:** The document characterizes payments as child support, spousal support/maintenance, or a property equalization payment, or contemplates property/retirement transfers or continuation of health insurance or other benefits, in ways whose tax or eligibility treatment a professional would evaluate.

**Why surface it:** How a payment, transfer, or benefit is characterized can carry tax and eligibility consequences that turn on current law and the parties' facts; the skill records the characterization the document uses, routes the question out, and states no result.

**What to record:**
- Record how each payment stream, property/retirement transfer, and benefits-continuation term is characterized, with section references, and note any recital about intended tax or eligibility treatment as written.
- Flag any payment whose characterization and label are inconsistent as `ambiguous`, and echo any election or continuation deadline verbatim (`[deadline verification required]`).

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Should the tax and benefits treatment of each payment, transfer, and benefit be confirmed with tax counsel and a benefits specialist `[Verify current law]`?
- Do the document's characterizations line up with how the payments and transfers actually function?

## 8. Settlement-Agreement Internal-Consistency Issues

These patterns are internal to the document. The skill surfaces inconsistencies and gaps for the attorney; it never resolves an ambiguity, chooses between readings, or supplies a missing term.

### 8.1 Undefined or inconsistently used defined terms

**Pattern:** A defined term is used but never defined, defined but never used, or used in two places with different apparent meanings; party labels or names are inconsistent across the document.

**Why surface it:** Undefined or inconsistently used terms are a leading source of later disputes over what an agreement means; the skill catalogs them and does not decide the intended meaning.

**What to record:**
- List each used-but-undefined and defined-but-unused term with section references, and note any term that appears to carry different meanings in different places as `ambiguous`.
- Flag inconsistent party labels, names, or role references.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- What was the intended meaning of each undefined or inconsistently used term?
- Do the party labels and names need to be conformed throughout?

---

### 8.2 Inconsistent or conflicting parenting schedules

**Pattern:** The parenting-time, holiday, vacation, and special-day provisions overlap, conflict, or leave gaps — a holiday schedule that contradicts the regular schedule, an unallocated block, or two provisions that both claim the same time.

**Why surface it:** Conflicting schedule provisions cannot all be administered at once and generate enforcement friction; the skill surfaces the conflict and the gap and does not decide which provision controls.

**What to record:**
- Identify each pair of provisions that overlap or conflict, with section references, and note any time period the schedule leaves unallocated as `not addressed`.
- Note whether a precedence rule (which schedule controls) is stated, or absent.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- Which provision is intended to control where the schedules conflict?
- Are the gaps and precedence questions resolved before the order is finalized?

---

### 8.3 Ambiguous support triggers and missing referenced attachments

**Pattern:** A support obligation depends on a triggering event, condition, or figure that is undefined or unclear; or the document references a schedule, exhibit, or attachment that is not present.

**Why surface it:** A support trigger open to more than one reading, or a missing referenced attachment, leaves part of the agreement indeterminate; the skill flags both and neither interprets the trigger nor reconstructs the attachment.

**What to record:**
- Quote each ambiguous support trigger or condition and the readings it is open to, with references, and echo any figure or date in a trigger verbatim.
- List every referenced schedule, exhibit, or attachment and mark any that is `not found`.

**Questions for the attorney:**
- What reading of each ambiguous trigger did the parties intend?
- Are all referenced schedules and exhibits present and consistent with the body of the document?

## Reviewer Notes

- A surfaced pattern is a prompt to look closely and to frame a question for the attorney — never a conclusion that a provision is unfair, unenforceable, or contrary to a child's interests.
- Nothing here scores a parent or spouse, assesses capacity or undue influence, decides a characterization, values a claim, computes support or a deadline, or predicts a court outcome. Those are all for the attorney.
- Section 5 patterns override the ordinary workflow: on any safety or vulnerable-party indicator, escalate immediately per `core/capacity-and-vulnerable-parties.md` before continuing, and route to `domestic-violence-safety-referral-checklist`.
- This catalog is not exhaustive. A document-specific or unusual pattern not listed here may still be worth surfacing under the nearest issue area, framed the same way — as facts to record and questions for counsel.
- See `skills/family-law/settlement-agreement-issue-spotter/SKILL.md` for the full settlement-agreement review across all issue areas, and `skills/family-law/custody-order-review-checklist/SKILL.md` for the custody- and parenting-order clarity review.
