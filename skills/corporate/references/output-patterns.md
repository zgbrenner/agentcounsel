# Corporate Output Patterns

This file collects the **common rules every corporate skill follows** in its Output Format — the cross-cutting discipline that should be true of board minutes, a written consent, a closing checklist, a diligence issues memo, a material-contract schedule, and an entity-compliance review alike. Each skill's own `SKILL.md` remains the authoritative spec for what that skill produces; this file is a consistency reference, not a substitute.

The corporate skills produce documents that, once attorney-approved, become **official records** of an entity — minutes, consents, schedules attached as exhibits to executed agreements, closing certificates, and good-standing inventories. The discipline below exists to keep drafts honest and to make the boundary between draft and official record explicit on every page.

---

## Universal rules

- **Every output is a draft.** Each deliverable opens with a clearly visible draft-for-attorney-review label. A draft minute set, a draft consent, a draft schedule, and a draft checklist all carry the same explicit not-yet-official posture.
- **Every output is not legal advice.** Output never tells a user that an action is duly authorized, that quorum was met, that a consent is effective, that a schedule is complete, that a closing condition is satisfied, or that an entity is in good standing.
- **Every action is recorded as the source states it.** Resolutions, votes, signatures, effective dates, attendance, board composition, share counts, and contract amounts come from the user's provided materials — minutes drafts, board books, cap tables, executed documents, diligence sets — and cite their source. Nothing is reconstructed from training memory.
- **No deadline is computed.** Filing deadlines, renewal dates, annual-report due dates, notice periods, anti-assignment windows, and statute-of-repose dates are recorded as the source states them and tagged `[deadline verification required]`.

## Source citation and definition discipline

Corporate deliverables frequently sit downstream of a transaction agreement, a charter, a bylaw, an operating agreement, or a stock-purchase agreement that **defines its own terms**. Outputs follow three rules.

- **The governing document's definition controls.** "Material Contract", "Subsidiary", "Affiliate", "Knowledge", and similar defined terms come from the agreement at hand, quoted verbatim with section reference. A skill does not import a market-standard definition or a definition from a different agreement.
- **Every entry on a schedule has a traceable source.** A contract on a material-contract schedule cites its data-room location or Bates reference. A consent on a closing checklist cites the agreement section that requires it. A signatory on a written consent cites the board resolution or charter provision that authorizes them.
- **Definitions that are ambiguous in the controlling document are flagged.** The skill does not silently apply the more or less favorable reading. The ambiguity is recorded with `[ATTORNEY TO CONFIRM: interpretation]`.

## Official-record framing

Corporate skills sit closer to "official record" than most of the library. Outputs follow two rules.

- **Drafts are visibly drafts.** A draft set of minutes labeled "**DRAFT — Not Yet Official Record — For Attorney Review**" cannot be mistaken for the executed record. A draft consent retains every `[CONFIRM]` and `[ACTION]` placeholder until the attorney removes them.
- **Internal work product is separated from the official-record document.** Notes, analysis, alternatives considered, and consent-tracking overlays that support the drafting are presented as separate, plainly labeled internal-work-product sections — not embedded in the body of the record document.

## What the outputs never do

- They never assert that quorum was present, that a vote was duly taken, that a consent is effective, that signatories are duly authorized, that a closing condition is satisfied, or that an entity is in good standing.
- They never compute filing, annual-report, franchise-tax, change-of-control, or anti-assignment deadlines.
- They never finalize, sign, file, deliver, or transmit a corporate record. Every deliverable goes through the Attorney Verification Checklist before it leaves the draft state.

All output is draft work product for attorney review.
