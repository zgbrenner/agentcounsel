# Tax Issue Catalog

This catalog lists fact patterns and issue categories a reviewer should scan
for when organizing a tax document or fact set for a qualified tax
professional. Each entry describes what to surface, what to record, and the
questions it raises for that professional to resolve.

Tax skills organize facts and frame questions; they do not conclude. Nothing
here classifies an entity, determines nexus or taxability, classifies a
worker, states a tax treatment or position, computes a tax, an allocation, a
withholding amount, a gain or loss, or a deadline, or predicts an audit or
controversy outcome. Every entry surfaces a pattern and ends in questions for
a qualified tax professional or attorney — never a conclusion, a filing
position, or a statement that a treatment is correct.

This catalog is a review aid, not tax advice and not a tax or legal
conclusion. Whether a surfaced pattern matters depends on the specific
language, the jurisdictions and taxing authorities involved, the facts the
user supplies, and professional judgment. Every surfaced item is a candidate
for professional evaluation.

---

## How to Use This Catalog

1. Locate the provision, form line, ledger entry, or fact in the material
   under review — or note its absence, itself worth surfacing in many tax
   contexts.
2. Compare the pattern against the actual language or the facts as stated.
   Quote language verbatim with its source citation (form line, schedule,
   section, or ledger row); label each fact as a provided source, a
   user-stated fact, or `not provided` / `ambiguous`.
3. Record the pattern in the skill's issue-catalog scan, with the source and
   a neutral note. Never resolve an ambiguity; surface it.
4. Carry the topic's **Questions for the tax professional** into the
   deliverable as open questions. Do not answer them.
5. Mask SSN, EIN, TIN, and account numbers by default wherever a fact touches
   one; a full value appears only where strictly necessary and expressly
   requested. This applies wherever this catalog handles identity or account
   facts — entity classification, worker classification, transaction
   diligence, documentation, and crypto/digital-asset records alike.

Mark every jurisdiction-dependent question `[verify jurisdiction]`, every
current-law question `[Verify current law]`, and every date or period
`[deadline verification required]`. Never invent a rate, a threshold, a form
number, a dollar figure, a day count, a percentage, or a citation.

## 1. Entity Classification & Election Issues

Facts organized for `entity-tax-classification-checklist`; the skill never
states a classification.

**1.1 Formation and governing-document facts.** Entity type, formation
jurisdiction, ownership structure, and management rights as stated in
formation documents, operating/partnership agreements, and bylaws. Record
each as stated and flag any mismatch between the document type and the
entity's apparent form. *Question:* does the formation record support the
classification currently claimed `[verify jurisdiction]`?

**1.2 Elections made, missed, or ambiguous.** Check-the-box, S-corporation,
QSub, partnership-representative, or other classification elections, with
effective dates (`[deadline verification required]`) and whether the filed
form or confirmation is present, `not found`, or inconsistent with current
filings. *Question:* was each election properly made, timely, and still in
effect?

**1.3 Ownership changes and eligibility facts.** Ownership changes, new
interest classes, or eligibility-relevant events (owner count, disqualifying
owner type), each dated (`[deadline verification required]`) and sourced.
*Question:* does the change affect eligibility for an existing election or
classification `[Verify current law]`?

**1.4 Foreign ownership and cross-border classification.** Foreign owners,
subsidiaries, or a foreign parent, with jurisdiction and any classification
or reporting form referenced; mask foreign TINs and account numbers. Route
detailed cross-border questions to Section 7. *Question:* does foreign
ownership change the classification analysis or trigger a reporting
obligation `[verify jurisdiction]`?

**1.5 Filing-history consistency.** The classification reflected on filed
returns, by period and source, compared against the governing documents and
any stated election, with a mismatch flagged `ambiguous`. *Question:* does
the filing history support the claimed classification, or suggest a
correction is needed?

## 2. Sales/Use Tax Nexus & Taxability Issues

Facts organized for `sales-use-tax-nexus-triage`; the skill never concludes
nexus, taxability, or a filing obligation.

**2.1 Physical-presence facts.** Offices, inventory, employees, contractors,
and property (owned or third-party, including fulfillment-center or
trade-show presence) by jurisdiction, with dates (`[deadline verification
required]`) and source, including presence that has since ended. *Question:*
does this presence create a nexus question in the jurisdiction `[verify
jurisdiction]`?

**2.2 Economic-nexus facts.** Sales volume and transaction-count activity by
jurisdiction and period, as reported or estimated, with source; note whether
any threshold figure is user-stated, document-drawn, or `not provided` —
never state or apply a threshold from memory. *Question:* does the reported
activity meet or approach the jurisdiction's economic-nexus threshold
`[verify jurisdiction]`?

**2.3 Marketplace-facilitator facts.** Each sales channel, whether a
facilitator is involved, and what the facilitator represents about its
collection (per agreement or notice), with source. *Question:* does
facilitator collection change the seller's own registration or filing
obligation in a jurisdiction `[verify jurisdiction]`?

**2.4 Product/service-type characterization.** Each product or service line
(tangible goods, digital goods, software as product or service), its
characterization as stated by the business, and any jurisdiction-specific
label used on invoices or filings. *Question:* how is each category treated
in the relevant jurisdictions `[verify jurisdiction]`, and are mixed bundles
addressed?

**2.5 Exemption- and resale-certificate facts.** Each exempt or
resale-treated sale, the certificate on file (or `not found`), its stated
expiration (`[deadline verification required]`), and whether it names the
correct buyer and jurisdiction. *Question:* is each certificate valid,
current, and properly matched to the transaction `[Verify current law]`?

## 3. Worker Classification Tax Issues

Facts organized for `employment-tax-worker-classification-intake`; the skill
never classifies a worker. Reference workers by role or group and mask full
SSNs/TINs unless a full identifier is expressly required.

**3.1 Behavioral-control facts.** Instructions, training, supervision, and
set hours or schedule, each as stated, with source, noting whether
documented or only described. *Question:* do these facts bear on
classification under the applicable standard `[verify jurisdiction]`, and is
actual practice consistent with the written agreement?

**3.2 Financial-control facts.** Investment in tools or equipment,
opportunity for profit or loss, exclusivity, and payment method and
regularity, each as stated, with source. *Question:* is the payment and
reimbursement pattern consistent with the worker's stated status?

**3.3 Relationship-type facts.** The written agreement's stated relationship
(contractor, employee, other), benefits offered or excluded, engagement
duration and renewal history, and how the work relates to the business's
regular operations, each with source. *Question:* do these facts support the
classification currently applied, and are similarly situated workers
classified consistently `[verify jurisdiction]`?

**3.4 Documentation and reporting-form facts.** The reporting form used per
worker or group (Form W-2, Form 1099), by period and source; any mismatch
between the form used and the underlying facts, flagged `ambiguous`.
*Question:* is the reporting-form treatment supported by the underlying
facts, and what documentation is missing?

## 4. Transaction Tax Diligence Categories

Categories organizing `transaction-tax-diligence-request-list` and broader
diligence workstreams; no exposure figure is stated in any category.

| Tax area | Typical facts to record | Question the category raises |
|---|---|---|
| Income tax | Filing jurisdictions, filing history, open audits, NOL/credit carryforwards as stated, group structure | Are all filing obligations and open exposures identified `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Sales/use tax | Nexus footprint (Section 2), exemption certificates on file, registration history | Are registrations current in every jurisdiction with nexus facts? |
| Payroll/employment tax | Worker classification facts (Section 3), payroll tax filings, benefit-plan withholding | Are classification and withholding practices consistent across the workforce? |
| Property tax | Owned/leased real and personal property by jurisdiction, assessment appeals pending | Are all taxable property items and pending appeals identified? |
| Transfer tax | Prior asset or entity transfers, stated transfer-tax treatment and filings | Were transfer taxes properly assessed and paid on prior transactions `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Unclaimed property | Dormant balances, escheatment filings by state, reporting history | Is the unclaimed-property reporting history complete across states of exposure? |
| Credits, incentives, NOLs/attributes | Carryforward figures and documented basis, credit agreements, clawback conditions | Are the attributes documented and subject to any change-of-control limitation `[Verify current law]`? |

Cite every figure in every row to its source document or state `not
provided`; never total, verify, or apply a limitation to a carryforward or
credit figure.

## 5. Tax Covenant & Indemnity Mechanics

A high-level scan aid generalizing what a transaction agreement's tax
covenants and indemnities commonly contain. The full line-by-line mapping —
covenant text, section citations, and negotiation points — belongs in
`tax-covenants-indemnities-review/SKILL.md`, whose workflow steps walk each
mechanic individually. Nothing here states enforceability, tax treatment, or
adequacy, computes exposure, or performs an allocation.

**5.1 Pre-/post-closing tax covenants.** Restrictions on the target's tax
conduct, elections, and filings before closing, and on post-closing actions
affecting pre-closing periods, each verbatim with the bound party and any
consent right. *Question:* are the restrictions adequate, and can
post-closing action by one side shift tax burden to the other?

**5.2 Straddle-period allocation facts.** The Straddle Period definition and
allocation method (closing-of-the-books, per-diem, hybrid) quoted verbatim,
and which taxes it applies to. **Never compute or illustrate an
allocation.** *Question:* is the method stated for every tax type?

**5.3 Transfer taxes and refund entitlements.** The responsible party for
transfer/stamp/documentary taxes and the refund-entitlement mechanics,
verbatim, including any carve-out (for example, refunds reflected in working
capital). *Question:* are these provisions complete and free of overlap with
the purchase-price adjustment?

**5.4 Contest control and cooperation.** Who controls a tax contest for
pre-closing, Straddle Period, and indemnified matters; participation and
consent mechanics; cooperation scope and stated retention periods
(`[deadline verification required]`). *Question:* do contest-control
provisions conflict with the general indemnity's claim-control provision?

**5.5 Survival and cap/basket interaction.** Survival periods, caps,
baskets, and exclusions for tax reps, covenants, and the indemnity, exactly
as stated (`[deadline verification required]`), compared to the general
regime. **Never compute an expiry date or exposure.** *Question:* does the
tax-specific regime create a gap or unintended overlap?

**5.6 Indemnity scope and definitional hooks.** The tax indemnity's scope,
its interaction with the general indemnity, and definitions such as
`Pre-Closing Taxes`, a tax prong of `Permitted Liens`, or `Accrued Taxes` in
the purchase-price adjustment, verbatim. *Question:* does the interaction
create a double-recovery risk or coverage gap?

## 6. Contract Tax Provision Issues

Facts organized for `tax-provision-review-checklist`; the skill never states
enforceability or tax treatment.

**6.1 Gross-up provisions.** The clause verbatim, its trigger, and any
formula referenced (recorded, never computed). *Question:* is the trigger
clear and consistent with the withholding provision it responds to?

**6.2 Withholding provisions.** The clause verbatim, any documentation
requirement (withholding certificate, treaty form), and whether required
forms are on file or `not found`. *Question:* is the obligation and its
documentation complete, and does a treaty position need verification
`[verify jurisdiction]`?

**6.3 VAT/GST provisions.** The clause verbatim, whether stated prices are
inclusive or exclusive, and any registration or invoicing requirement
referenced. *Question:* is the treatment consistent across pricing,
invoicing, and payment provisions `[verify jurisdiction]`?

**6.4 Information-reporting provisions.** The reporting requirement
verbatim, stated delivery timing (`[deadline verification required]`), and
whether the form is on file or `not found`; mask any TIN or account number
the form would disclose. *Question:* is the required form on file, current,
and consistent with the payee's stated tax status?

## 7. International/Cross-Border Issues

Facts organized for `international-tax-issue-spotter` as **questions
only**; the skill never states a withholding rate, a permanent-establishment
conclusion, a transfer-pricing result, a treaty position, or a CFC/PFIC
outcome.

| Issue area | Facts to record | Question for tax counsel |
|---|---|---|
| Withholding | Payment type, payee jurisdiction, any treaty form or certificate on file (mask TINs) | Is the withholding rate and documentation correct for this payment and payee `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Permanent establishment | Physical presence, personnel, and activities in a foreign jurisdiction, as described | Do these facts raise a permanent-establishment question `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Transfer pricing | Intercompany transactions, stated pricing methodology, supporting documentation status | Is the intercompany pricing supported by contemporaneous documentation `[Verify current law]`? |
| Treaty questions | Countries involved, any treaty position claimed, supporting residency certification | Is the claimed treaty position supported and current `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| CFC/PFIC questions | Ownership percentages, entity classification, income-type facts, as stated | Do these ownership and income facts raise a CFC or PFIC question `[Verify current law]`? |

Every row is a routing question for tax counsel, never an answer. Record
only the facts the user or a provided document supplies.

## 8. Crypto/Digital Asset Issues

Facts organized for `crypto-digital-asset-tax-intake`; the skill never
calculates gain or loss, cost basis, or states tax treatment.

**8.1 Activity categorization.** Purchases, sales, swaps, staking, mining,
airdrops, NFT transactions, DeFi activity, or token grants, by category,
date (`[deadline verification required]`), and source (wallet or exchange
export, masked account reference). *Question:* is each activity correctly
categorized, and are all activity types captured `[Verify current law]`?

**8.2 Business-versus-personal characterization.** The user's
characterization of an activity, or `ambiguous` where unclear (for example,
mining or staking at scale), with the facts bearing on it. *Question:* does
the activity's scale and pattern support the stated characterization?

**8.3 Records and cost-basis documentation.** Which records (cost-basis
statements, wallet exports) are complete, partial, or `not provided` per
activity category, with masked account/wallet references as source.
*Question:* what records are missing before gain, loss, or income can be
evaluated for each category?

**8.4 Valuation and timing facts.** The stated value, its source, and
valuation timestamp as recorded, flagged `ambiguous` where sources conflict.
**Never compute a value.** *Question:* which valuation source and
methodology should govern, applied consistently across activities?

## 9. Documentation & Recordkeeping

Facts organized for `tax-document-organizer`.

**9.1 Document inventory completeness.** Returns, K-1s, W-2s, 1099s,
notices, audit correspondence, entity documents, payroll records, sales-tax
filings, certificates, and ledgers, by period, with completeness status
(complete/partial/illegible/`not provided`) and a masked, non-sensitive
reference in place of the document itself. *Question:* what is missing, and
does a gap affect a filing position or an issue surfaced elsewhere?

**9.2 Sensitive-identifier handling.** Whether SSNs, EINs, TINs, or account
numbers appear, using a masked form (for example, `EIN ••-•••1234`) rather
than the full value; flag any document that cannot be reviewed without the
full identifier. *Question:* is the full identifier actually needed for this
review step?

**9.3 Notice and correspondence facts.** The notice type (audit,
deficiency, or other agency correspondence), its stated date and any
response deadline (`[deadline verification required]`), and whether a
response has been prepared or filed. *Question:* has this notice been
escalated for immediate professional attention, and is the deadline
confirmed against the agency's own records?

**9.4 Retention and destruction facts.** The retention period a policy or
record schedule states for each document category (`[deadline verification
required]`), and whether any document was destroyed before that period
elapsed. *Question:* does the retention practice match the period the
applicable rule requires `[verify jurisdiction]`, and does any destroyed
document affect an open issue?

## 10. Cross-Cutting Jurisdiction & Multi-State Routing

Cross-cutting patterns to watch for regardless of which document is under
review.

**10.1 Multi-state or multi-country footprint inconsistency.** A
jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction summary of registration status against the
nexus and presence facts already recorded (Sections 1-3), flagging
mismatches `ambiguous`. *Question:* which jurisdictions need a registration
or filing review before other work proceeds `[verify jurisdiction]`?

**10.2 Routing to the correct specialist.** Issues surfaced in one section
(a cross-border fact in 1.4, a CFC/PFIC question in Section 7) that may need
a different specialist than the one handling the primary review, with the
section each came from. *Question:* has each cross-referenced issue been
routed to the right specialist, and is any deadline among them
time-sensitive?

**10.3 Apportionment and multi-state allocation facts.** The apportionment
factors (property, payroll, sales) a return or workpaper states by state,
with source, and any state using a different methodology than the others.
Never compute an apportionment percentage. *Question:* is each state's
apportionment methodology correctly applied and consistent with where the
underlying activity actually occurs `[verify jurisdiction]`?

## Reviewer Notes

- A surfaced pattern is a prompt to look closely and to frame a question for
  the tax professional — never a conclusion that a classification, a nexus
  determination, a taxability position, a worker's status, a treatment, or an
  exposure figure is correct.
- Nothing here computes a tax, an allocation, a withholding amount, a gain or
  loss, a cost basis, or a deadline. Those are all for the qualified tax
  professional or attorney.
- Mask SSNs, EINs, TINs, and account numbers by default in every
  identifier-bearing section (entity classification, worker classification,
  transaction diligence, documentation, and crypto/digital-asset records); a
  full value appears only where strictly necessary and expressly requested.
- This catalog is not exhaustive. A document-specific or unusual pattern not
  listed here may still be worth surfacing under the nearest issue area,
  framed the same way — as facts to record and questions for the tax
  professional.
- See the individual `SKILL.md` files in `skills/tax/` for the full review
  workflow each skill runs; this catalog supplies the pattern content that
  fills the tables defined in `skills/tax/references/output-patterns.md`.
