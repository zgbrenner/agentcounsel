# Tax Issue Catalog

This catalog lists fact patterns and issue categories a reviewer should scan
for when organizing a tax document or fact set for a qualified tax
professional. Each entry describes what to surface, what to record, and the
questions it raises for that professional to resolve.

Tax skills organize facts and frame questions; they do not conclude. Nothing
here classifies an entity, determines nexus or taxability, classifies a
worker, states a tax treatment or position, computes a tax, an allocation, a
withholding amount, a gain or loss, or a deadline, or predicts an audit or
controversy outcome. Every entry surfaces a pattern and ends in questions for
a qualified tax professional or attorney — never a conclusion, a filing
position, or a statement that a treatment is correct.

This catalog is a review aid, not tax advice and not a tax or legal
conclusion. Whether a surfaced pattern matters depends on the specific
language, the jurisdictions and taxing authorities involved, the facts the
user supplies, and professional judgment. Every surfaced item is a candidate
for professional evaluation.

---

## How to Use This Catalog

For each pattern or topic below:

1. Locate the provision, form line, ledger entry, or fact in the material
   under review — or note its absence, itself worth surfacing in many tax
   contexts.
2. Compare the pattern against the actual language or the facts as the user
   stated them. Quote language verbatim with its source citation (form line,
   schedule, section, or ledger row); label each fact as a provided source, a
   user-stated fact, or `not provided` / `ambiguous`.
3. Record the pattern in the skill's issue-catalog scan, with the source and
   a neutral note. Never resolve an ambiguity; surface it.
4. Carry the topic's **Questions for the tax professional** into the
   deliverable as open questions. Do not answer them.
5. Mask SSN, EIN, TIN, and account numbers by default wherever a fact touches
   one; a full value appears only where strictly necessary and expressly
   requested. This applies in every section below that handles identity or
   account facts — entity classification, worker classification, transaction
   diligence, documentation, and crypto/digital-asset records alike.

Mark every jurisdiction-dependent question `[verify jurisdiction]`, every
current-law question `[Verify current law]`, and every date or period
`[deadline verification required]`. Never invent a rate, a threshold, a form
number, a dollar figure, a day count, a percentage, or a citation.

## 1. Entity Classification & Election Issues

Classification facts are organized for `entity-tax-classification-checklist`;
the skill never states a classification.

### 1.1 Formation and governing-document facts

**Pattern:** Formation documents, operating or partnership agreements, and
bylaws state (or leave unclear) the entity type, jurisdiction of formation,
ownership structure, and management rights.

**What to record:** The entity type and formation jurisdiction as stated, the
governing documents reviewed, and any inconsistency between the document type
and the entity's apparent form.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does the formation record support the
classification the entity or its filings currently claim? Are the governing
documents complete and internally consistent `[verify jurisdiction]`?

---

### 1.2 Elections made, missed, or ambiguous

**Pattern:** The entity's history references a check-the-box election, an
S-corporation election, a QSub election, a partnership-representative
designation, or another classification-relevant election, with the election
form, effective date, or acceptance/confirmation letter missing or
inconsistent.

**What to record:** Each election referenced, its stated effective date
(`[deadline verification required]`), and whether the filed form or
confirmation is present, `not found`, or inconsistent with the entity's
current filings.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Was each election properly made,
timely, and still in effect? Does a missing or defective election create a
classification question that needs correction?

---

### 1.3 Ownership changes and eligibility facts

**Pattern:** A change in ownership, a new class of interest, or an
eligibility-relevant event (number or type of owners, a disqualifying owner)
occurs, with the effect on any existing election left unaddressed.

**What to record:** Each ownership change as stated, its date
(`[deadline verification required]`), and the source (cap table, transfer
ledger, amendment). Note any owner type (trust, nonresident, entity) that an
eligibility rule may turn on.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does the change affect eligibility
for an existing election or classification `[Verify current law]`? Is a new
filing or disclosure required?

---

### 1.4 Foreign ownership and cross-border classification questions

**Pattern:** The entity has one or more foreign owners, foreign
subsidiaries, or a foreign parent, and classification, withholding, or
information-reporting consequences are unaddressed.

**What to record:** Each foreign owner or affiliate as stated, its
jurisdiction, and any classification or reporting form referenced (mask
foreign TINs and account numbers by default). Route detailed cross-border
questions per Section 7.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does foreign ownership change the
classification analysis or trigger a reporting obligation `[verify
jurisdiction]`? Are the required informational filings identified?

---

### 1.5 Filing-history consistency

**Pattern:** The entity's tax return history (entity type reported, EIN used,
elections claimed) does not plainly match the governing documents or a
stated election.

**What to record:** The classification reflected on filed returns, with
period and source, and any mismatch against the governing documents or an
election, flagged `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does the filing history support the
claimed classification, or does it suggest a correction or an inadvertent
election is needed?

---

## 2. Sales/Use Tax Nexus & Taxability Issues

Nexus and taxability facts are organized for `sales-use-tax-nexus-triage`;
the skill never concludes nexus, taxability, or a filing obligation.

### 2.1 Physical-presence facts

**Pattern:** The business has offices, inventory, employees, contractors, or
property (owned or third-party, including fulfillment-center or trade-show
presence) located in a jurisdiction.

**What to record:** Each physical-presence fact by jurisdiction, its dates
(`[deadline verification required]`), and source. Note any presence that has
since ended and when.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does this presence create a nexus
question in the jurisdiction `[verify jurisdiction]`? Is the presence fully
inventoried across all jurisdictions the business touches?

---

### 2.2 Economic-nexus facts

**Pattern:** The business has sales volume or transaction-count activity in
a jurisdiction that may approach or cross an economic-nexus threshold, with
the applicable threshold and measurement period unconfirmed.

**What to record:** Sales and transaction-count figures by jurisdiction and
period, as reported or estimated, with source, and whether any threshold
figure is user-stated, drawn from a document, or `not provided`. Never state
or apply a threshold number from memory.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does the reported activity meet or
approach the jurisdiction's economic-nexus threshold `[verify
jurisdiction]`? Are the sales figures complete and consistent across
systems?

---

### 2.3 Marketplace-facilitator facts

**Pattern:** Some or all sales are made through a marketplace facilitator
that may collect and remit tax on the seller's behalf, with the scope of
facilitator-collected sales versus direct sales unclear.

**What to record:** Each sales channel, whether a facilitator is involved,
and what the facilitator represents about its collection (as stated in an
agreement or notice), with source.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does facilitator collection change
the seller's own filing or registration obligation in a jurisdiction `[verify
jurisdiction]`? Is the facilitator's collection scope documented and
consistent with actual sales channels?

---

### 2.4 Product- and service-type characterization

**Pattern:** The business sells tangible goods, digital goods, software (as
a product or a service), or services, and a jurisdiction's tax treatment can
turn on which category applies — a distinction the facts do not always make
clear.

**What to record:** Each product or service line, its characterization as
stated by the business, and any jurisdiction-specific label the business
uses on invoices or filings.

**Questions for the tax professional:** How is each product or service
category treated in the relevant jurisdictions `[verify jurisdiction]`? Are
mixed bundles (goods plus services, software plus support) separately
addressed?

---

### 2.5 Exemption- and resale-certificate facts

**Pattern:** Sales are treated as exempt or resale-exempt, with the
supporting certificate absent, expired, or not matched to the specific
transaction or jurisdiction.

**What to record:** Each exempt or resale-treated sale, the certificate on
file (or `not found`), its stated expiration (`[deadline verification
required]`), and whether it names the correct buyer and jurisdiction.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is each exemption or resale
certificate valid, current, and properly matched to the transaction `[Verify
current law]`? What is the exposure from unsupported exempt sales?

---

## 3. Worker Classification Tax Issues

Worker facts are organized for
`employment-tax-worker-classification-intake`; the skill never classifies a
worker. Mask worker names and full SSNs/TINs by referencing workers by role
or group unless a full identifier is expressly required.

### 3.1 Behavioral-control facts

**Pattern:** The engagement facts touch on control over how, when, and where
the work is performed — instructions, training, supervision, and set hours
or schedule.

**What to record:** Each control-related fact as stated, with source (a
contract clause, a policy, a described practice), and whether it is
documented or only described.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Do these facts bear on worker
classification under the applicable standard `[verify jurisdiction]`? Are
there gaps in documentation of actual practice versus the written
agreement?

---

### 3.2 Financial-control facts

**Pattern:** The engagement facts touch on investment in tools or
equipment, opportunity for profit or loss, exclusivity, method and
regularity of payment, and expense reimbursement.

**What to record:** Each financial-control fact as stated, with source, and
whether payment is by project, invoice, or a regular wage-like schedule.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Do these facts bear on worker
classification under the applicable standard `[verify jurisdiction]`? Is the
payment and reimbursement pattern consistent with the worker's stated
status?

---

### 3.3 Relationship-type facts

**Pattern:** A written agreement, benefits eligibility, engagement duration,
and whether the work performed is central to the business's regular
operations are stated or implied.

**What to record:** The written agreement's stated relationship (contractor,
employee, other), benefits offered or excluded, engagement duration and any
renewal history, and how the work relates to the business's regular
operations, each with source.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Do these relationship facts support
the classification the business currently applies `[verify jurisdiction]`?
Are there multiple workers in materially the same role classified
differently?

---

### 3.4 Documentation and reporting-form facts

**Pattern:** Form W-2 or Form 1099 treatment, payroll records, and any
independent-contractor agreement do not plainly match the facts recorded
above, or documentation for a worker or group is incomplete.

**What to record:** The reporting form used per worker or group, by period,
with source; any mismatch between the form used and the underlying facts,
flagged `ambiguous`. Mask full SSNs/TINs; reference workers by role or group.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is the reporting-form treatment
supported by the underlying facts? What documentation is missing before the
classification question can be evaluated?

## 4. Transaction Tax Diligence Categories

These categories organize `transaction-tax-diligence-request-list` and the
broader diligence workstreams; the skill states no exposure figure and
reaches no conclusion in any category.

| Tax area | Typical facts to record | Question the category raises |
|---|---|---|
| Income tax | Filing jurisdictions, filing history, open audits, NOL/credit carryforwards as stated, group structure | Are all filing obligations and open exposures identified `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Sales/use tax | Nexus footprint (Section 2), exemption certificates on file, registration history | Are registrations current in every jurisdiction with nexus facts? |
| Payroll/employment tax | Worker classification facts (Section 3), payroll tax filings, benefit-plan withholding | Are classification and withholding practices consistent across the workforce? |
| Property tax | Owned/leased real and personal property by jurisdiction, assessment appeals pending | Are all taxable property items and pending appeals identified? |
| Transfer tax | Prior asset or entity transfers, stated transfer-tax treatment and filings | Were transfer taxes properly assessed and paid on prior transactions `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Unclaimed property | Dormant balances, escheatment filings by state, reporting history | Is the unclaimed-property reporting history complete across states of exposure? |
| Credits, incentives, and NOLs/attributes | Carryforward figures and their documented basis, credit agreements, clawback conditions | Are the attributes properly documented and subject to any change-of-control limitation `[Verify current law]`? |

For each row, cite every figure to its source document or state `not
provided`; never total, verify, or apply a limitation to a carryforward or
credit figure.

## 5. Tax Covenant & Indemnity Mechanics

This section generalizes, at a high level, the mechanics a transaction
agreement's tax covenants and indemnities commonly contain. It is a scan aid
for surfacing the topic in any tax review; the full line-by-line mapping —
covenant text, section citations, and negotiation points — belongs in
`tax-covenants-indemnities-review/SKILL.md`, whose workflow steps walk each
mechanic individually. Nothing here states enforceability, tax treatment, or
adequacy, computes exposure, or performs an allocation.

### 5.1 Pre-closing and post-closing tax covenants

**Pattern:** Covenants restrict the target's tax conduct, elections, and
return filings before closing, and restrict post-closing actions (elections,
amended returns) that could affect pre-closing periods.

**What to record:** Each covenant verbatim, who is bound, and any consent
right, with section reference.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Are the restrictions and consent
rights adequate, and do post-closing actions by one side shift tax burden to
the other?

---

### 5.2 Straddle-period allocation facts

**Pattern:** A Straddle Period definition and an allocation method
(closing-of-the-books, per-diem, hybrid) are stated for dividing tax
liability between pre- and post-closing periods.

**What to record:** The allocation method quoted verbatim, and which taxes
it applies to, with section reference. **Never compute or illustrate an
allocation.**

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is the method stated for every tax
type, and is it applied consistently with the agreement's other definitions?

---

### 5.3 Transfer taxes and refund entitlements

**Pattern:** The agreement allocates responsibility for transfer, stamp, or
documentary taxes, and states who is entitled to refunds or credits for
pre-closing periods.

**What to record:** The responsible party for transfer taxes and the
refund-entitlement mechanics, verbatim, with section reference; note any
carve-out (for example, refunds reflected in working capital).

**Questions for the tax professional:** Are transfer-tax and refund
provisions complete and free of gaps or overlap with the purchase-price
adjustment?

---

### 5.4 Contest control and cooperation

**Pattern:** The agreement designates who controls a tax contest for
pre-closing, Straddle Period, and indemnified matters, and sets cooperation
and records-access obligations.

**What to record:** The control, participation, and consent mechanics
verbatim, and the cooperation scope and stated retention periods
(`[deadline verification required]`), with section reference.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Do the contest-control provisions
conflict with the general indemnity's claim-control provision, and is
cooperation adequate?

---

### 5.5 Survival and cap/basket interaction

**Pattern:** Tax reps, tax covenants, and the tax indemnity carry survival
periods, caps, baskets, and exclusions that may differ from the agreement's
general indemnity regime.

**What to record:** Each period and limitation exactly as stated
(`[deadline verification required]`), and how the tax-specific regime
compares to the general regime, with section reference. **Never compute an
expiry date or exposure.**

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does the tax-specific survival and
cap/basket regime create a gap or an unintended overlap with the general
regime?

---

### 5.6 Indemnity scope and definitional hooks

**Pattern:** The tax indemnity's scope, its interaction with the general
indemnity, and definitions such as `Pre-Closing Taxes`, a tax prong of
`Permitted Liens`, or an `Accrued Taxes` concept in the purchase-price
adjustment, may overlap or conflict.

**What to record:** Each definition and the indemnity scope verbatim, with
section reference, and how they interact as drafted.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does the interaction create a
double-recovery risk or a gap in coverage?

---

## 6. Contract Tax Provision Issues

Contract-level tax provisions are organized for
`tax-provision-review-checklist`; the skill never states enforceability or
tax treatment.

### 6.1 Gross-up provisions

**Pattern:** A payment clause requires a gross-up so the recipient receives
a stated net amount after withholding or another tax, with the trigger and
calculation method described in varying detail.

**What to record:** The gross-up clause verbatim, its trigger, and any
formula referenced (recorded, never computed), with section reference.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is the gross-up trigger and
mechanic clear and consistent with the withholding provision it responds
to?

---

### 6.2 Withholding provisions

**Pattern:** A payment clause addresses withholding tax — who withholds, on
what basis, and what documentation (a withholding certificate, a treaty
form) is required to reduce or eliminate it.

**What to record:** The withholding clause verbatim, any documentation
requirement, and whether the required forms are on file or `not found`, with
section reference.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is the withholding obligation and
its documentation complete, and does a treaty position require
verification `[verify jurisdiction]`?

---

### 6.3 VAT/GST provisions

**Pattern:** A pricing or invoicing clause addresses VAT, GST, or a similar
value-added tax — whether stated prices are inclusive or exclusive, and who
bears the tax.

**What to record:** The VAT/GST clause verbatim, whether prices are stated
inclusive or exclusive, and any registration or invoicing requirement
referenced, with section reference.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is the VAT/GST treatment consistent
across the pricing, invoicing, and payment provisions `[verify
jurisdiction]`?

---

### 6.4 Information-reporting provisions

**Pattern:** A clause requires an information return, a withholding
certificate, or another reporting form (for example, a Form W-9/W-8 series
equivalent) to be delivered, with the delivery timing and consequence of
non-delivery described.

**What to record:** The reporting requirement verbatim, the stated delivery
timing (`[deadline verification required]`), and whether the form is on file
or `not found`, with section reference. Mask any TIN or account number the
form would disclose.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is the required reporting form on
file, current, and consistent with the payee's stated tax status?

---

## 7. International/Cross-Border Issues

International facts are organized for `international-tax-issue-spotter` as
**questions only**; the skill never states a withholding rate, a permanent-
establishment conclusion, a transfer-pricing result, a treaty position, or a
CFC/PFIC outcome.

| Issue area | Facts to record | Question for tax counsel |
|---|---|---|
| Withholding | Payment type, payee jurisdiction, any treaty form or certificate on file (mask TINs) | Is the withholding rate and documentation correct for this payment and payee `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Permanent establishment | Physical presence, personnel, and activities in a foreign jurisdiction, as described | Do these facts raise a permanent-establishment question `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| Transfer pricing | Intercompany transactions, stated pricing methodology, and supporting documentation status | Is the intercompany pricing supported by contemporaneous documentation `[Verify current law]`? |
| Treaty questions | Countries involved, any treaty position claimed, and supporting residency certification | Is the claimed treaty position supported and current `[verify jurisdiction]`? |
| CFC/PFIC questions | Ownership percentages, entity classification, and income-type facts, as stated | Do these ownership and income facts raise a CFC or PFIC question `[Verify current law]`? |

Every row is a routing question for tax counsel, never an answer. Record
only the facts the user or a provided document supplies.

## 8. Crypto/Digital Asset Issues

Digital-asset facts are organized for `crypto-digital-asset-tax-intake`; the
skill never calculates gain or loss, cost basis, or states tax treatment.

### 8.1 Activity categorization

**Pattern:** Records show purchases, sales, swaps, staking, mining,
airdrops, NFT transactions, DeFi activity, or a token grant, without a clear
categorization.

**What to record:** Each activity by category, date (`[deadline
verification required]`), and source (wallet or exchange export, masked
account reference).

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is each activity correctly
categorized for tax purposes, and are all activity types captured `[Verify
current law]`?

---

### 8.2 Business-versus-personal characterization

**Pattern:** The user characterizes an activity as business or personal, or
the facts leave the characterization unclear (for example, mining or
staking conducted at scale).

**What to record:** The characterization as the user states it, or
`ambiguous`, with the facts bearing on it.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Does the activity's scale and
pattern support the stated characterization?

---

### 8.3 Records and cost-basis documentation

**Pattern:** Cost-basis records, exchange statements, or wallet exports are
incomplete, from multiple sources with inconsistent figures, or absent for
a category of activity.

**What to record:** Which records are complete, partial, or `not provided`
per activity category, with masked account/wallet references as the source.

**Questions for the tax professional:** What records are missing before
gain, loss, or income can be evaluated for each activity category?

---

### 8.4 Valuation and timing facts

**Pattern:** A transaction's value or timing is stated inconsistently
across sources, or a valuation methodology (spot price, exchange rate at a
stated time) is unclear.

**What to record:** The stated value, its source, and the valuation
timestamp as recorded, flagged `ambiguous` where sources conflict. Never
compute a value.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Which valuation source and
methodology should govern, and is it applied consistently across
activities?

---

## 9. Documentation & Recordkeeping

Documentation facts are organized for `tax-document-organizer`.

### 9.1 Document inventory completeness

**Pattern:** Returns, K-1s, W-2s, 1099s, notices, audit correspondence,
entity documents, payroll records, sales-tax filings, certificates, and
ledgers are inventoried, with gaps by period or document type.

**What to record:** Each document, its period, and completeness status
(complete/partial/illegible/`not provided`), with a masked, non-sensitive
reference in place of the document itself.

**Questions for the tax professional:** What documents are missing, and
does a gap affect a filing position or an open issue elsewhere in the
review?

---

### 9.2 Sensitive-identifier handling

**Pattern:** A document or record set contains SSNs, EINs, TINs, or account
numbers.

**What to record:** Whether sensitive identifiers appear, using a masked
form (for example, `EIN ••-•••1234`) rather than reproducing the full
value; flag any document that cannot be reviewed without the full identifier.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Is the full identifier actually
needed for this review step, or is a masked reference sufficient?

---

### 9.3 Notice and correspondence facts

**Pattern:** An audit notice, deficiency notice, or other agency
correspondence is on file, with a stated response date.

**What to record:** The notice type, its stated date and any response
deadline (`[deadline verification required]`), and whether a response has
been prepared or filed.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Has this notice been escalated for
immediate professional attention, and is the response deadline confirmed
against the agency's own records?

---

## 10. Cross-Cutting Jurisdiction & Multi-State Routing

Multi-jurisdiction facts recur across every tax skill; this section collects
cross-cutting patterns to watch for regardless of which document is under
review.

### 10.1 Multi-state or multi-country footprint inconsistency

**Pattern:** The entity's registrations, filings, or nexus facts (Sections
1-3) are inconsistent across jurisdictions — registered in some
jurisdictions with nexus facts but not others, or vice versa.

**What to record:** A jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction summary of registration
status against the nexus and presence facts already recorded, flagging
mismatches `ambiguous`.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Which jurisdictions need a
registration or filing review before other work proceeds `[verify
jurisdiction]`?

---

### 10.2 Routing to the correct specialist

**Pattern:** An issue surfaced in one section (for example, a
cross-border fact in Section 1.4, or a CFC/PFIC question in Section 7) may
require a different specialist than the one handling the primary review.

**What to record:** Each issue that appears to need routing, and to which
practice area or specialist (income tax, state and local tax, international
tax, benefits counsel), with the section it came from.

**Questions for the tax professional:** Has each cross-referenced issue been
routed to the right specialist, and is any deadline among them
time-sensitive?

## Reviewer Notes

- A surfaced pattern is a prompt to look closely and to frame a question for
  the tax professional — never a conclusion that a classification, a nexus
  determination, a taxability position, a worker's status, a treatment, or an
  exposure figure is correct.
- Nothing here computes a tax, an allocation, a withholding amount, a gain or
  loss, a cost basis, or a deadline. Those are all for the qualified tax
  professional or attorney.
- Mask SSNs, EINs, TINs, and account numbers by default in every
  identifier-bearing section (entity classification, worker classification,
  transaction diligence, documentation, and crypto/digital-asset records); a
  full value appears only where strictly necessary and expressly requested.
- This catalog is not exhaustive. A document-specific or unusual pattern not
  listed here may still be worth surfacing under the nearest issue area,
  framed the same way — as facts to record and questions for the tax
  professional.
- See the individual `SKILL.md` files in `skills/tax/` for the full review
  workflow each skill runs; this catalog supplies the pattern content that
  fills the tables defined in `skills/tax/references/output-patterns.md`.
