## Severity Key

### Legal Risk

| Rating | Definition |
|---|---|
| **High** | Material risk of significant financial loss, liability exposure, loss of IP rights, regulatory violation, or loss of key contractual protection. Requires attorney attention before execution. |
| **Medium** | Meaningful but manageable risk; could become High depending on business context or deal value. Recommend addressing in negotiation. |
| **Low** | Minor imbalance or suboptimal language; acceptable in many contexts but worth flagging. |

### Business Friction

| Rating | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Blocking** | The term, as written, would prevent or fundamentally undermine the client's intended use of the contract or the underlying business relationship. |
| **Slowing** | The term creates operational drag — extra process, approval steps, costs, or constraints — that would meaningfully burden the client but does not block the relationship. |
| **Confusing** | The term is ambiguous, undefined, or inconsistent in a way that creates uncertainty but may have no immediate practical effect. |
| **None** | The term has no material practical effect on the client's operations, even if it carries legal risk. |

_Note: Legal Risk and Business Friction are rated independently. A term can be legally enforceable yet operationally benign (High legal risk / None business friction), or legally marginal yet disruptive in practice (Low legal risk / Blocking business friction)._
