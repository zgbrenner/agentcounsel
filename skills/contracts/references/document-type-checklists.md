# Document-Type Review Checklists

These checklists identify the must-check items specific to each contract type. They supplement the general clause-by-clause review in `skills/contracts/contract-risk-review/SKILL.md` — they do not replace it. Use these checklists alongside the full risk matrix to ensure document-type-specific issues are not missed.

Each checklist focuses on what is distinctive about that document type: what is present that should be scrutinized carefully, what is commonly absent that may be a gap, and what interactions with other documents or obligations must be confirmed.

All findings are draft legal work product for attorney review. This material is not legal advice and not a final negotiating position.

---

## How to Use These Checklists

1. Identify the document type being reviewed.
2. Work through the relevant checklist before or alongside the general clause-by-clause review.
3. Use checklist items to inform the risk matrix and issue list.
4. Add any item not addressed in the contract to the Missing Provisions section.
5. Flag all attorney-verification items in the Open Items section.

If the document does not fit neatly into one category (e.g., an MSA with an embedded SaaS subscription), apply the checklists for all applicable types and reconcile any conflicts.

## 1. Master Services Agreement (MSA)

An MSA is a framework agreement governing an ongoing services relationship. It typically contemplates multiple statements of work or work orders that incorporate its terms. The MSA is the backbone of the relationship; gaps here affect every project under it.

### Structural and Relationship Items

- [ ] Is the MSA intended to govern multiple engagements or only a single project? If multiple, confirm that the scope is broad enough.
- [ ] Is the relationship between the MSA and subordinate documents (SOWs, work orders, purchase orders) clearly defined? Which controls in case of conflict?
- [ ] Are affiliates and subsidiaries covered — as service recipients, obligors, or both?
- [ ] Is the relationship properly characterized as independent contractor, not employment or joint venture?

### Services and Scope

- [ ] How are services defined — by reference to SOWs only, or in the MSA itself? If by SOW reference only, confirm the MSA contains adequate protections even before any SOW is executed.
- [ ] Is there a change-order or change-management mechanism?
- [ ] Who controls the scope of work — is it the client's direction or the vendor's determination?
- [ ] Are deliverable acceptance standards defined, or is acceptance at vendor's discretion?

### Intellectual Property

- [ ] Does the MSA define who owns work product created under each SOW, or does ownership depend on SOW-level terms?
- [ ] If work product is assigned to the client, does the assignment language use the present tense? (See `skills/contracts/references/red-flags.md`, Section 3.1.)
- [ ] Is there a license-back for vendor background IP embedded in deliverables?
- [ ] Does the MSA address moral rights? `[verify jurisdiction]`
- [ ] Is there a further-assurances obligation?

### Confidentiality

- [ ] Does the MSA contain a confidentiality provision, or is it expected to appear only in an NDA?
- [ ] If both an MSA and a separate NDA are in place, confirm which governs and whether there are any inconsistencies.
- [ ] Does the confidentiality term survive expiration of the MSA and termination of individual SOWs?

### Limitation of Liability and Indemnification

- [ ] Is the liability allocation expressed per-SOW, per-contract, or in aggregate across all SOWs?
- [ ] If the cap is fee-based, is it calculated from all SOW fees, only the relevant SOW's fees, or a fixed amount?
- [ ] Are the carve-outs from the cap consistent with what the parties need for protection across all engagement types?

### Termination and Transition

- [ ] Can the MSA be terminated independently of active SOWs, or do active SOWs survive MSA termination?
- [ ] Is there a wind-down mechanism for in-flight projects on termination?
- [ ] Is there a transition assistance obligation?

### Governance

- [ ] Are there escalation, key-personnel, or governance provisions for managing the ongoing relationship?
- [ ] Are audit rights or reporting requirements present?
- [ ] Is there a most-favored pricing provision? If so, see `skills/contracts/references/red-flags.md`, Section 11.3.

## 2. NDA / Confidentiality Agreement

For NDA review, the primary workflow is `skills/contracts/nda-review/SKILL.md`. This checklist identifies the structural must-checks for completeness.

### Identity and Structure

- [ ] Are the parties correctly identified (legal entities, not just trade names)?
- [ ] Is the NDA unilateral, bilateral, or mutual? Does the structure match the label?
- [ ] Is the NDA stand-alone or embedded in a larger transaction (M&A, employment, investment)? If embedded, route to specialist counsel.

### Definition of Confidential Information

- [ ] Is Confidential Information defined? Is the definition appropriately scoped for the disclosing party's needs?
- [ ] Are oral disclosures covered? If so, what confirmation procedure applies?
- [ ] Are standard exclusions present (publicly available, independently developed, received from third parties without restriction, required to be disclosed by law or court order)?
- [ ] Is there a residual-knowledge clause? If so, see `skills/contracts/references/red-flags.md`, Section 3.5.

### Permitted Use and Non-Use

- [ ] Is the permitted purpose defined narrowly enough to match the intended transaction?
- [ ] Is there a non-use obligation (not just non-disclosure)?
- [ ] Are there restrictions on who within the receiving party may access Confidential Information?
- [ ] Are there controls on disclosure to the receiving party's representatives (attorneys, accountants, advisors)?

### Term and Survival

- [ ] What is the NDA term?
- [ ] How long do confidentiality obligations survive expiration or termination?
- [ ] Is there an indefinite or longer survival period for trade secrets or specifically identified categories?

### Return and Destruction

- [ ] Is there a return-or-destroy obligation?
- [ ] Is there a certification requirement on destruction?
- [ ] Are backup copies, legal hold exceptions, and regulatory retention requirements addressed?

### Remedies

- [ ] Is there a right to seek injunctive relief for breach?
- [ ] Is injunctive relief available in a court, or is it subject to a mandatory arbitration clause?
- [ ] Are remedies limited to a cap, or is the cap expressly excluded for confidentiality breaches?

### Scope Check (Non-Confidentiality Obligations)

- [ ] Is there a standstill clause?
- [ ] Is there a non-solicitation clause?
- [ ] Is there a non-compete clause?
- [ ] Is there an exclusivity clause?
- [ ] Is there an IP assignment or licensing provision?
- [ ] Is there a right of first refusal or most-favored-nation clause?
- [ ] Any "yes" answer to the above means the document is more than an NDA: raise the triage rating and flag for attorney review.

## 3. Statement of Work / Work Order

A statement of work (SOW) or work order is a project-specific document executed under an MSA or similar framework agreement. It defines what is being done, by when, for how much, and to what standard. SOW terms are often less carefully reviewed than the MSA, but they create the specific obligations that generate liability.

### Scope and Deliverables

- [ ] Are deliverables defined with sufficient specificity to know when the vendor has performed?
- [ ] Are acceptance criteria defined? Who determines acceptance — the client, the vendor, or an objective standard?
- [ ] If there is no acceptance procedure, consider what "deemed acceptance" may occur by default.

### Timeline and Milestones

- [ ] Are deadlines and milestones expressly stated?
- [ ] Are any deadlines conditions to payment, deliverable hand-off, or other obligations?
- [ ] Are there consequences for missing milestones (delay damages, termination rights, reduced fees)?
- [ ] Note: never compute or confirm a deadline — flag all dates for attorney verification. `[deadline verification required]`

### Fees and Payment

- [ ] Are fees fixed, time-and-materials, or milestone-based? Is the fee structure clearly defined?
- [ ] Are payment milestones tied to deliverable acceptance, calendar dates, or both?
- [ ] Are out-of-pocket expense reimbursements addressed, and is there a pre-approval requirement?
- [ ] Are change-order fees expressly carved out from the fixed price?

### Personnel and Key Persons

- [ ] Are specific personnel identified as key to performance?
- [ ] Is there a restriction on replacing key personnel without client consent?
- [ ] Are there subcontracting restrictions?

### Intellectual Property

- [ ] Does the SOW specify who owns the work product, or does it defer entirely to the MSA?
- [ ] If the SOW creates custom deliverables, confirm the MSA's IP assignment covers them.
- [ ] Are third-party components (open source, licensed tools) identified and addressed?

### Consistency with the Governing MSA

- [ ] Does the SOW conflict with any MSA term (liability, IP, confidentiality, termination)?
- [ ] Which document controls in case of conflict? Is there an order-of-precedence clause, and where does the SOW fall?
- [ ] Is the SOW properly executed as a contract modification if it departs from the MSA?

## 4. Software or SaaS License / Subscription Agreement

SaaS and software subscription agreements carry a distinct risk profile because the client often receives no transferable asset — only a right to access a service that can be modified, interrupted, or terminated.

### License or Access Grant

- [ ] Is the grant a software license, a service access right, or both? The distinction matters for what the client receives on termination.
- [ ] Is the scope of permitted use clearly defined (users, devices, volume, field of use, territory)?
- [ ] Is the right to use affiliates or subsidiaries addressed?
- [ ] Are there restrictions on copying, modifying, or reverse-engineering the software?

### Auto-Renewal and Pricing

- [ ] Is there an auto-renewal clause? What is the non-renewal notice window? (See `skills/contracts/references/red-flags.md`, Section 9.)
- [ ] Does the vendor have a unilateral right to increase fees at renewal or during the term?
- [ ] If price escalation is permitted, is it capped?
- [ ] Does the client have a termination right if it rejects a price increase?

### Service Levels

- [ ] Are uptime commitments or service levels defined?
- [ ] What are the remedies for failure to meet service levels (credits, termination, nothing)?
- [ ] Are maintenance windows, scheduled downtime, and emergency downtime addressed?
- [ ] Are service levels limited to certain infrastructure or service components, excluding others?

### Data Terms

- [ ] Who owns client data? Is this expressly stated?
- [ ] What are the vendor's permitted uses of client data?
- [ ] Is there a data processing agreement or addendum?
- [ ] What are the data deletion and portability rights on termination?
- [ ] If the service involves AI or machine learning, does the vendor's data use right extend to training models on client data? (Route to `ai-vendor-terms-review` if applicable.)

### Updates, Modifications, and Discontinuation

- [ ] Can the vendor modify the software or service unilaterally? With what notice?
- [ ] Can the vendor discontinue features the client relies on?
- [ ] Is there a commitment to support legacy versions or provide advance notice before sunsetting?

### Termination and Data Exit

- [ ] What happens to client data on termination? Is there a retrieval period?
- [ ] Is there a data export right, and in what format?
- [ ] Is there a transition assistance obligation?
- [ ] Is access cut off immediately on termination, or is there a wind-down period?

### Vendor Warranties and Disclaimers

- [ ] What warranties does the vendor make about software performance or fitness for purpose?
- [ ] Is there a broad warranty disclaimer? Does it leave the client with no meaningful recourse for non-conformance?
- [ ] Are there specific warranties about security, compliance, or regulatory certifications?

## 5. Vendor or Supplier Agreement

A vendor or supplier agreement governs the purchase of goods, raw materials, components, or commodity services. The primary risks are often supply continuity, product quality, price, and liability for defective goods.

### Supply Commitment and Forecasting

- [ ] Is the vendor obligated to supply specific quantities, or is the agreement a framework with no firm commitment?
- [ ] Are there minimum purchase requirements on the client's side?
- [ ] Are forecasting, purchase order, and lead-time mechanics clearly defined?
- [ ] Are there force majeure or allocation provisions covering supply disruptions?

### Quality and Specifications

- [ ] Are product specifications defined by reference to a document (specification sheet, datasheet, incorporated exhibit)?
- [ ] Is there an inspection and rejection right for non-conforming goods?
- [ ] Is there a cure or replacement obligation for rejected goods?
- [ ] Are there warranty periods for goods after delivery or acceptance?

### Price, Escalation, and Most-Favored Pricing

- [ ] Is pricing fixed for the term, or subject to escalation?
- [ ] If escalation is permitted, is it capped or tied to an objective index?
- [ ] Is there a most-favored-customer pricing commitment? (See `skills/contracts/references/red-flags.md`, Section 11.3.)

### Supply Continuity and Business Continuity

- [ ] Is there a last-time-buy notice obligation if the vendor discontinues a product?
- [ ] Is there a business continuity or disaster recovery obligation?
- [ ] Is there an obligation to maintain minimum inventory levels or safety stock?

### Intellectual Property

- [ ] If the goods include proprietary technology or software, are license terms addressed?
- [ ] Does the client have any rights to product improvements or custom modifications?
- [ ] Are there any IP indemnification obligations running from vendor to client for third-party IP claims?

### Termination and Transition

- [ ] Is there a termination-for-convenience right, and does it allow adequate wind-down time?
- [ ] What happens to open purchase orders on termination?
- [ ] Is there a transition period for finding an alternative supplier?

## 6. Professional Services Agreement

A professional services agreement (PSA) governs engagements with service providers — consultants, advisors, specialists, or agencies. Key risks center on scope creep, IP ownership, personnel, and liability.

### Scope of Services

- [ ] Is the scope of services specific enough to know what performance looks like?
- [ ] Is there a change-order mechanism, or can the scope expand without additional compensation?
- [ ] Are deliverables or outputs defined, or is the engagement purely time-and-materials?
- [ ] Are there exclusivity or non-compete restrictions on the service provider?

### Personnel and Staffing

- [ ] Are key personnel identified? Is there a consent requirement for replacement?
- [ ] Is subcontracting permitted? With what controls?
- [ ] Is the service provider's relationship with its staff (independent contractors vs. employees) addressed? `[verify jurisdiction]`

### Intellectual Property

- [ ] Who owns materials, analyses, reports, and other work product created under the PSA?
- [ ] Is the IP assignment in present-tense language? (See `skills/contracts/references/red-flags.md`, Section 3.1.)
- [ ] Is there a license-back for pre-existing service-provider tools, methodologies, or templates embedded in deliverables?
- [ ] Are there any third-party content or open-source components that affect the IP chain of title?

### Representations and Standard of Care

- [ ] What standard of care does the service provider commit to (reasonable professional standard, best efforts, specific outcome)?
- [ ] Are there representations about qualifications, licenses, or certifications?
- [ ] Are representations and warranties qualified by a knowledge standard where appropriate?

### Conflict of Interest and Non-Solicitation

- [ ] Is there a conflict-of-interest representation and ongoing obligation?
- [ ] Is there a non-solicitation of employees or customers provision?
- [ ] If non-solicitation covers the client's employees, is the scope and duration reasonable? `[verify jurisdiction]`

### Liability and Professional Liability Insurance

- [ ] Does the PSA require professional liability (errors and omissions) insurance?
- [ ] Is the liability cap sized appropriately relative to the engagement fee and potential harm from professional error?
- [ ] Are there any carve-outs from the liability cap for gross negligence, fraud, or willful misconduct?

### Termination

- [ ] Is there a termination-for-convenience right with reasonable notice?
- [ ] What are the fees payable on termination — only work completed, or all projected fees?
- [ ] Are there transition obligations (handover of work product, documentation, data)?

## Reviewer Notes

- These checklists are organized by document type, but many agreements combine elements of multiple types. Apply all relevant checklists and note any overlaps or conflicts.
- The absence of a term listed in a checklist is a finding — record it in the Missing Provisions section of the risk matrix.
- The checklists are a starting point. Document-specific or deal-specific issues may not appear on any checklist. Use the "Unusual or One-Sided Clauses" category to capture anything that does not fit the standard categories.
- Jurisdiction-specific requirements (e.g., mandatory clauses, regulated forms, consumer protection terms) are not assessed by these checklists and must be confirmed by the attorney `[verify jurisdiction]`.
