# Employment Output Patterns

This file collects the **common rules every employment skill follows** in its Output Format — the cross-cutting discipline that should be true of a termination risk assessment, a worker-classification analysis, a hiring review, a severance review, a policy review, an investigation memo, a protected-leave review, and a wage-and-hour question alike. Each skill's own `SKILL.md` remains the authoritative spec for what that skill produces; this file is a consistency reference, not a substitute.

Employment skills produce different artifacts (a risk table, an analytical memo, an issues table, a checklist), but they share an operating discipline.

---

## Universal rules

- **Every output is a draft.** Each deliverable opens with a clearly visible draft-for-attorney-review label.
- **Every output is not legal advice.** Each deliverable carries a not-legal-advice notice. Output never tells a manager whether to fire, hire, classify, or pay a worker; never tells an employee whether they have a claim; never asserts what an agency or court will conclude.
- **Jurisdiction is named on every output.** Employment law is state-, city-, and country-specific. Each output records the work jurisdiction (or `[verify jurisdiction]`), the residency jurisdiction if different, and any cross-jurisdictional question.
- **No deadline is computed.** Notice periods, COBRA windows, FMLA windows, ADEA review windows, statutes of limitation, and final-pay deadlines are recorded as the source states them and tagged `[deadline verification required]`. The skill never tells a user "you have N days."
- **Protected-class and protected-activity inferences are flagged, never asserted.** A skill notes a fact that may bear on protected status or protected activity; it does not conclude whether the fact creates discrimination, retaliation, or interference exposure.

## Sensitive-content handling

Employment matters often touch protected categories, medical conditions, accommodation requests, and complaints of harassment, discrimination, or retaliation. Outputs follow three rules.

- **Sensitive categories are recorded only where the user has supplied them.** A skill does not infer protected status, medical status, age, sexual orientation, religion, or national origin from indirect cues.
- **Allegations are recorded as allegations.** An internal-investigation output records who said what, who heard what, and what document supports it — not who is right.
- **Personal identifiers are not embedded into reusable templates.** Names, dates of birth, social security numbers, and medical detail used to populate a draft are not stored in any reusable copy of a template.

## Risk-framing rules

When a deliverable rates risk (termination risk, severance acceptance risk, leave-interference risk), three rules apply.

- **Risk ratings are workflow signals, not legal conclusions.** A "High" rating means the supervising attorney should examine the issue; it does not mean the action is unlawful. A "Low" rating means no flag from the workflow; it does not mean the action is safe.
- **Severity floors.** Once an issue has been rated High, that rating is not silently downgraded. Any reduction in severity is an explicit attorney decision recorded as such.
- **Adverse facts are surfaced even where the user is the employer or the employee.** The skill names facts that are unfavorable to the user's position; it does not draft from the user's preferred conclusion backwards.

## What the outputs never do

- They never tell a user whether to terminate, hire, classify, accommodate, deny, or grant.
- They never compute COBRA, FMLA, ADEA, final-pay, or statute-of-limitations dates.
- They never assert that a worker is or is not an employee, exempt, eligible, qualified, or in a protected class.
- They never approve a separation agreement, a release, a policy, a hiring offer, or an investigation outcome for finalization — every deliverable goes through the Attorney Verification Checklist before it leaves the draft state.

All output is draft work product for attorney review.
