## 3. Issue List (Buyer perspective)

- **B-1. Indemnity cap equals the escrow amount.** Sellers' aggregate
  liability is capped at the Escrow Amount of $1,000,000 (§ 8.3, p. 31) —
  roughly 2.4% of the $42,000,000 price. *Concern:* a cap set at the escrow
  effectively limits Buyer's recourse to the escrow fund and removes recourse
  against the Sellers directly. *Suggested direction:* toward a higher
  indemnity cap, and toward Buyer recourse beyond the escrow for breaches of
  fundamental representations, with the escrow as security rather than as the
  ceiling.
- **B-2. Short survival period for representations.** The § 4 reps survive
  only 12 months (§ 8.4, p. 32). *Concern:* many breaches surface only after
  a full operating cycle or an audit; a 12-month survival can expire before
  Buyer discovers a problem. *Suggested direction:* toward a longer general
  survival period, and toward longer or indefinite survival for fundamental
  reps and for tax-related reps. `[deadline verification required]`.
- **B-3. Sellers control the closing statement; short objection window.**
  Sellers prepare the closing statement, which is "final and binding" unless
  Buyer objects within 10 days (§ 2.4, p. 8). *Concern:* the preparing party
  controls the figures and the window is short for Buyer to review.
  *Suggested direction:* toward Buyer (or a jointly engaged accountant)
  preparing the statement, a longer objection window, Buyer access to
  supporting records, and a defined independent-accountant dispute process.
  `[deadline verification required]`.
- **B-4. Exclusive-remedy clause with no carve-outs.** Indemnification under
  § 8.2 is stated as Buyer's "sole and exclusive remedy" (§ 8.2, p. 30).
  *Concern:* with no carve-outs, this can foreclose Buyer's recourse for
  matters such as fraud or willful breach. *Suggested direction:* toward
  carve-outs from exclusivity (for example, for fraud, willful misconduct,
  and specific performance of covenants) — described as a direction only;
  whether and how to limit remedies is a legal question for counsel.
- **B-5. Knowledge qualifier across the representations.** The § 4 reps are
  each given "to the Sellers' Knowledge" (§ 4, p. 15). *Concern:* a blanket
  knowledge qualifier shifts the risk of unknown problems to Buyer.
  *Suggested direction:* toward removing the knowledge qualifier from core
  and fundamental reps, and toward a defined "Knowledge" standard that
  includes constructive knowledge after reasonable inquiry.
- **B-6. Reps not expressly tied to disclosure schedules.** Section 4 does
  not reference disclosure schedules in its body (§ 4, p. 15), though
  schedules are referenced generally elsewhere. *Concern:* unclear scope of
  what qualifies the reps; general or undisclosed schedule disclosures can
  erode Buyer's protections. *Suggested direction:* toward clear, section-
  specific schedule cross-references and away from general disclosure that
  qualifies all reps. The disclosure schedules were not provided and must be
  obtained and reviewed.
- **B-7. Indemnity triggers limited to § 4 rep breaches.** Section 8.2
  addresses indemnification for "breaches of the representations in § 4"
  (§ 8.2, p. 30); no indemnity for covenant breaches, pre-closing taxes, or
  specified matters was located. *Concern:* gaps in Buyer's post-closing
  recourse. *Suggested direction:* toward expanding indemnifiable matters to
  cover covenant breaches, pre-closing taxes, and any known special-
  indemnity items. This review reaches no conclusion on tax treatment.
- **B-8. No Seller restrictive covenants located.** No non-compete or
  non-solicit by the Sellers was found. *Concern:* the Sellers could compete
  with or solicit from the acquired business after Closing. *Suggested
  direction:* toward adding Seller non-compete and non-solicit covenants of
  appropriate scope and duration. `[verify jurisdiction]` — enforceability
  and permissible scope of restrictive covenants are jurisdiction-specific
  legal questions for counsel.

## 5. Negotiation Points (Buyer perspective)

1. **Raise the indemnity cap and decouple it from the escrow** (§ 8.3,
   p. 31) — direction: a higher cap with the escrow as security, plus
   uncapped or higher-capped recourse for fundamental reps.
2. **Extend survival periods** (§ 8.4, p. 32) — direction: longer general
   survival; longer or indefinite survival for fundamental and tax reps.
3. **Rebalance the purchase-price adjustment** (§ 2.4, p. 8) — direction:
   Buyer or joint preparation of the closing statement, a longer objection
   window, records access, and a defined independent-accountant process.
4. **Add carve-outs to the exclusive-remedy clause** (§ 8.2, p. 30) —
   direction only; counsel to determine scope.
5. **Narrow the knowledge qualifier** (§ 4, p. 15) — direction: remove from
   fundamental reps; define "Knowledge."
6. **Add Seller restrictive covenants** — direction: non-compete and
   non-solicit of appropriate scope and duration; `[verify jurisdiction]`.
7. **Preserve the absence of a financing condition** (§ 6.2, p. 24) —
   Buyer-favorable as drafted; resist any Seller attempt to add one only if
   that aligns with Buyer's actual funding certainty `[CONFIRM]`.
8. **Add a pro-sandbagging provision** — none was located; direction: a
   provision preserving Buyer's indemnity claims regardless of pre-closing
   knowledge, subject to counsel's view and `[verify jurisdiction]`.
