## 4. Issue List and Negotiation Checklist (Buyer perspective)

**High priority**

1. **Undefined working-capital adjustment.** § 1 references a "customary"
   adjustment with no target, mechanic, or dispute process. *Concern:* an
   open mechanic invites a post-closing price dispute. *Suggested direction:*
   negotiate a defined working-capital target, a true-up procedure, and an
   independent-accountant dispute path in the definitive agreement.
2. **Escrow and indemnity scope undefined.** § 6 sets a 10% / 18-month
   escrow but is silent on indemnity caps, baskets, deductibles, and
   survival periods. *Concern:* the escrow alone does not define Buyer's
   recovery rights. *Suggested direction:* reserve full indemnity
   architecture for the definitive agreement; consider whether 10% is
   adequate given undiagnosed diligence risk.
3. **Confidentiality references an NDA not provided.** § 4 makes
   confidentiality binding but ties it to a mutual NDA that was not
   supplied. *Concern:* Buyer cannot assess its own obligations. *Suggested
   direction:* obtain and review the NDA before countersigning.

**Medium priority**

4. **Diligence access is vague.** § 5 grants "reasonable access" without
   defined scope or timing. *Concern:* access disputes can compress the
   diligence window. *Suggested direction:* specify scope, categories, and
   response timeframes.
5. **Financing not addressed.** The Term Sheet is silent on financing.
   *Concern:* Buyer's funding path and any financing condition are
   undocumented. *Suggested direction:* confirm internally whether the deal
   is fully funded and whether a financing condition should be raised.
6. **Exclusivity duration.** § 3 sets a 60-day no-shop. *Concern:* 60 days
   may be tight for full diligence and definitive-agreement drafting.
   *Suggested direction:* consider an automatic short extension if diligence
   is progressing in good faith. `[deadline verification required]`.

**Low priority**

7. **Forum-selection clause absent.** § 8 names governing law but no forum.
   *Suggested direction:* add a forum-selection clause in the definitive
   agreement.
