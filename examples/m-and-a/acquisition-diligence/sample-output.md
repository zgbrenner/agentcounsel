# Sample Output — Acquisition Diligence Request List

> **Illustrative example — not legal advice.** This is a sample of what the Acquisition Diligence Request List skill (`skills/m-and-a/acquisition-diligence-request-list/SKILL.md`) produces, run against the fictional request in `sample-request.md`. Every party, company, date, and figure is **fictional** and was invented for illustration; there are no real entities and no real or invented legal citations. It is the kind of draft work product a supervising attorney would review — not a finished deliverable, and not legal advice. See `examples/README.md`.

## 1. Deal Summary

- **Deal type:** Stock purchase — Buyer to acquire 100% of the outstanding
  shares of the Target.
- **Side this list is built for:** Buyer-side (Northwind Capital Partners, LLC).
- **Industry:** B2B subscription software — a hosted cloud analytics platform
  sold to mid-market customers.
- **Target profile:** Brightpath Analytics, Inc., a privately held software
  company; roughly 140 employees (about 60 in engineering); revenue
  predominantly recurring SaaS subscriptions; third-party cloud hosting;
  open-source components in the product; an option pool and one prior preferred
  financing round.
- **Transaction stage:** Post-LOI confirmatory diligence; pre-signing.
- **Jurisdiction:** Target's jurisdiction of incorporation and jurisdiction of
  operations both `[CONFIRM: jurisdiction]` `[verify jurisdiction]`; customer
  and personnel locations not yet enumerated.
- **Known risks / focus areas:** customer concentration; data privacy and
  security; open-source software; equity and option-pool cleanliness.

This list is a **draft scope for attorney review** — a scaffold for the deal
team to adjust. It is not the legally required scope of diligence, and it is not
a statement that completing it makes diligence sufficient. The reviewing
attorney decides what the list must add or drop and when diligence is enough.
