# Sample Request — Acquisition Diligence Request List

> *Fictional sample request.* The parties, company, deal facts, and figures
> below were invented purely to illustrate how the Acquisition Diligence
> Request List skill (`skills/m-and-a/acquisition-diligence-request-list/SKILL.md`)
> is invoked. Nothing here describes a real matter.

## The request, in plain language

"We're on the buy side of an acquisition and we're about to open confirmatory
diligence. Can you build us a tailored due-diligence request list, organized by
workstream, so the deal team has a scaffold for what to ask the target to
produce? We'd like each item to carry a priority, a short rationale, who owns
it, and the follow-up questions the produced material should answer. We already
have a few areas we want to probe hard."

## Fictional inputs

- **Deal type:** Stock purchase — the buyer is acquiring 100% of the
  outstanding shares of the target.
- **Side:** Buyer-side. The list is to be built from the buyer's perspective.
- **Transaction stage:** Post-LOI confirmatory diligence; the parties have
  signed a non-binding letter of intent and are not yet at signing.
- **Industry:** B2B software — a subscription analytics platform sold to
  mid-market customers, delivered as a hosted cloud service.
- **Jurisdiction:** A U.S. state of incorporation and a U.S. state of principal
  operations, both to be confirmed; stated generically here as "the target's
  jurisdiction of incorporation and its jurisdiction of operations"
  `[verify jurisdiction]`. Customers and personnel may sit in additional
  jurisdictions, not yet enumerated.

### Fictional target profile

- **Target (fictional):** Brightpath Analytics, Inc. ("Brightpath" or the
  "Target") — a privately held software company.
- **Buyer (fictional):** Northwind Capital Partners, LLC ("Buyer").
- **Approximate size:** Roughly 140 employees; about 60 of them in engineering.
- **Business:** Develops and hosts a subscription analytics product; revenue is
  predominantly recurring SaaS subscriptions on annual contracts.
- **Distinguishing features:** Processes meaningful volumes of customer data;
  uses a third-party cloud-hosting provider; engineering relies on open-source
  components; equity includes an employee option pool and one prior preferred
  financing round.

### Known risk areas the buyer wants to probe

- **Customer concentration** — the buyer believes a small number of accounts
  may drive a large share of recurring revenue.
- **Data privacy and security** — the Target handles customer data and has had
  no formal security audit that the buyer is aware of.
- **Open-source software** — the buyer wants visibility into open-source
  licenses embedded in the product.
- **Equity and option-pool cleanliness** — the buyer wants the capitalization
  table and option grants confirmed before signing.

The buyer notes that any timing, contingency dates, or closing conditions are
governed by the LOI and definitive documents and have not been independently
confirmed.
