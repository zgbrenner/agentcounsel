# Sample Request — estate-document-summary

> Fictional illustration. All parties, facts, figures, and documents below are
> invented for this example.

Please run **estate-document-summary** on a trust document and summarize the
key terms for the attorney.

**Matter facts**

- The user's role: associate attorney reviewing a client's existing documents.
- Jurisdiction: the document states it is governed by the law of State B.
- Review purpose: summarize the trust before a planned amendment discussion.

**Materials provided**

- The Hartwell Family Revocable Trust (fictional), Articles 1–9.
- One trust amendment (fictional), the First Amendment.

**Document references (fictional)**

- Article 1 — grantor: Eleanor Hartwell; trustee: Eleanor Hartwell; successor
  trustee: a fictional trust company.
- Article 3 — revocability and amendment language.
- Article 4 — dispositive provisions: distributions to two named children.
- Article 5 — trustee powers.
- Article 7 — a no-contest provision.
- Article 9 — governing law (State B); execution block with notary and two
  witnesses.
- The First Amendment changes the successor trustee.

**Stated facts**

- The associate wants the key terms, fiduciaries, dispositive provisions, and
  any ambiguities flagged.
- The original trust's signature page is partially illegible on the copy
  provided.

Please: cite the fictional articles (for example, `Trust Art. 4`); use
`unknown / not found / not provided / ambiguous` for gaps; do **not** conclude
validity, capacity, or enforceability; and flag ambiguities without resolving
them.
