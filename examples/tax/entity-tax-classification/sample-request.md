> Fictional illustration. All parties, facts, figures, and documents below are
> invented for this example.

Please run **entity-tax-classification-checklist** and organize the entity facts
for our tax counsel.

- Entity: Meadowbrook Ventures LLC, a limited liability company (fictional).
- Jurisdiction of formation: State A.
- Tax year/period: 2024 forward.
- Our role: in-house counsel, organizing facts before engaging tax counsel.
- Review purpose: a recent ownership change and a contemplated election make tax
  classification a live question for counsel.

**Materials provided**

- Operating Agreement excerpt (fictional), §2 (membership) and §4 (management).
- Membership Ledger (fictional), 1 page.
- A draft election form (fictional) — signature and date fields blank.

**Stated facts**

- The LLC began 2024 as single-member (one founder).
- In mid-2024 two new members were admitted, including one non-U.S. individual
  (per Membership Ledger).
- The Operating Agreement is silent on tax classification.
- The founder mentions wanting to "elect corporate treatment" but the draft
  election form is unsigned and undated.
- No tax filings have been made for the entity yet.

Please: build an entity classification facts table; list documents tax counsel
should review; frame classification questions; use `unknown / not found /
not provided / ambiguous` for gaps; do **not** conclude classification,
election validity, or tax status.
