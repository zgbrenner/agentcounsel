> Fictional illustration. All parties, facts, figures, forms, and documents
> below are invented for this example. The example contains no real or invented
> legal authority.

Please run **insurance-policy-summary** on a commercial general liability
policy and prepare a source-cited summary for counsel.

**Matter facts**

- Named insured: Maple Ridge Cabinetry LLC (fictional).
- The user's role: in-house counsel for the named insured.
- Policy type: commercial general liability (CGL).
- Review purpose: a coverage review on a pending third-party claim.
- Jurisdiction: governing law `not provided`.

**Materials provided (fictional policy document set)**

- Declarations page, p.1 — named insured, policy number, policy period, and a
  schedule of limits.
- Coverage Form CG-EX-1 (fictional) — the insuring agreements, definitions,
  exclusions, and conditions.
- Endorsement E-1 (fictional) — an additional insured endorsement referencing a
  schedule.
- Endorsement E-2 (fictional) — a per-project aggregate endorsement.
- A forms schedule listing the forms above.

**Stated facts**

- The declarations show a policy period and per-occurrence and aggregate
  limits.
- The user has not located a separate professional services coverage part and
  is unsure whether the policy includes one.

Please: build a policy overview, a key terms table, a coverage parts table, an
exclusions/conditions inventory, and an endorsements table; cite every item to
the page, form, or endorsement; mark anything you cannot locate as `not found`;
treat the policy period as `[deadline verification required]`; and do **not**
conclude that any coverage exists.
