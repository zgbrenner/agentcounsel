# Sample Request — Real Estate Diligence Checklist (Acquisition)

> *Fictional sample request.* The parties, property, documents, and facts below
> were invented purely to illustrate how the Real Estate Diligence Checklist
> skill (`skills/real-estate/real-estate-diligence-checklist/SKILL.md`) is
> invoked. Nothing here describes a real matter.

## The request, in plain language

"We're under contract to acquire a multi-tenant retail center and we're opening
the diligence period this week. We're the buyer. Can you build us a tailored
due-diligence checklist organized by category so the deal team has a scaffold
to work from, and a list of what we still need to request from the seller? We
have some documents already but a lot is still outstanding."

## Fictional inputs

- **Transaction type:** Acquisition (asset purchase of the property).
- **Property type:** Multi-tenant retail center (a neighborhood shopping center
  with roughly a dozen in-line tenants and one anchor space).
- **Jurisdiction:** A U.S. state and county to be confirmed; stated generically
  here as "the state and county where the property is located"
  `[verify jurisdiction]`.
- **Party role:** Buyer.
- **Parties (fictional):**
  - Buyer — Lark Hollow Retail Partners, LLC.
  - Seller — Cedarpoint Property Holdings, LLC.
- **Property (fictional):** "Maple Crossing," a multi-tenant retail center; no
  street address provided.

### Document set available so far

- Fully executed purchase and sale agreement (the "PSA").
- A preliminary rent roll (one page, undated).
- A title commitment from a prior owner's refinancing, age unknown.

### Documents the buyer says are still outstanding

- The tenant leases, amendments, and any guaranties.
- A current survey and the title exception documents.
- Environmental reports (Phase I and any prior assessments).
- Service and vendor contracts.
- Operating statements and the trailing income-and-expense history.
- Real estate tax bills and any assessment notices.
- Certificates of occupancy and permit records.
- Entity and authority documents for the seller.

The buyer notes the diligence period and any contingency dates are governed by
the PSA and have not been independently confirmed.
