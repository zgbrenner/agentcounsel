## 3. Clause-by-Clause Issue List (Tenant perspective)

- **Rent and economic terms.** Base Rent is $28.00/RSF/annum in Year 1 with
  a fixed 4% annual escalation (§ 3.1, p. 4). The 4% compounding escalator
  is above a flat or CPI-linked structure and increases Tenant's long-term
  cost. Operating Expenses pass through with **no base year and no expense
  stop**, and expressly include capital expenditures and Landlord's
  management fee (§ 4.2, p. 7; Exhibit C, p. 2). No audit right is stated.
  *Risk to Tenant:* uncapped, unverifiable expense exposure. *Direction:*
  toward a CPI cap or lower fixed escalator; a base year or expense stop; a
  cap on controllable expenses; exclusion or amortization of capital items;
  and an audit right.
- **Use.** Permitted use is "general office purposes and no other use
  whatsoever" (§ 6.3, p. 11). *Risk to Tenant:* a narrow clause may
  constrain future business changes and assignment marketability.
  *Direction:* toward broader "general office and any related lawful use"
  language.
- **Exclusivity.** `Not addressed` in the excerpts provided — typical for
  office space; absence is not itself a Tenant risk here.
- **Co-tenancy.** `Not addressed` — typical for office; not a material
  Tenant risk.
- **Go-dark.** `Not addressed` — office leases generally omit this; not a
  material Tenant risk.
- **Assignment and subletting.** Consent required, and Landlord may withhold
  it in its "sole and absolute discretion" (§ 9.1, p. 16). *Risk to
  Tenant:* limits exit flexibility, M&A activity, and the ability to sublet
  excess space. *Direction:* toward a "not unreasonably withheld" standard,
  permitted transfers to affiliates and in connection with a
  merger/sale, and removal of any recapture right `[CONFIRM: recapture not
  located]`.
- **Maintenance and repair.** Tenant must maintain **and replace** the HVAC
  system at its sole cost (§ 12.4, p. 21). *Risk to Tenant:* full capital
  replacement exposure for a major building system. *Direction:* toward
  Landlord-borne replacement, or a cap with amortization of any
  replacement over its useful life.
- **Casualty and condemnation.** `Ambiguous / Not found` — no provision was
  located in the copy provided. This is a material gap: a lease normally
  addresses rent abatement and termination after a fire or taking.
  `[CONFIRM: locate casualty and condemnation provisions]`.
- **Default and remedies.** A monetary default arises if any sum is unpaid
  when due, with a **three-day** cure period after written notice (§ 15.2,

## 6. Prioritized Issue List

**High priority**

1. **Uncapped operating expenses with no base year.** OpEx passes through in
   full, includes capital expenditures and the management fee, and carries
   no audit right (§ 4.2, p. 7; Exhibit C, p. 2). *Why it matters:* open-
   ended, unverifiable cost growth over a seven-year term. *Suggested
   Direction:* introduce a base year or expense stop, cap controllable
   expenses, exclude or amortize capital items, and add an audit right.
2. **Sole-discretion assignment consent.** § 9.1 (p. 16) lets Landlord
   withhold consent for any reason. *Why it matters:* blocks subletting
   excess space and complicates a future sale or merger of Tenant.
   *Suggested Direction:* move to a reasonableness standard and add
   permitted affiliate/M&A transfers.
3. **Tenant-borne HVAC replacement.** § 12.4 (p. 21) puts capital
   replacement of a major system on Tenant. *Why it matters:* a single
   replacement can be a large unbudgeted cost. *Suggested Direction:* shift
   replacement to Landlord or cap and amortize Tenant's share.
4. **Missing casualty and condemnation terms.** No provision located.
   *Why it matters:* without abatement and termination rights, Tenant may
   keep paying rent on unusable space. *Suggested Direction:* locate the
   provisions; if genuinely absent, negotiate abatement and termination
   rights.

**Medium priority**

5. **Three-day monetary cure period.** § 15.2 (p. 27). *Why it matters:* an
   administrative slip can mature into a default. *Suggested Direction:*
   lengthen the cure period and confirm notice must precede the clock.
6. **Renewal at undefined "market rent."** § 19.1 (p. 33). *Why it matters:*
   an undefined mechanism gives Landlord pricing leverage. *Suggested
   Direction:* define an objective rent-setting process.
7. **Narrow permitted use.** § 6.3 (p. 11). *Why it matters:* limits
   business changes and assignability. *Suggested Direction:* broaden the
   use clause.

**Low priority**

8. Compounding 4% escalator (§ 3.1, p. 4) — economic, not legal; raise as a
   business point.
