# Fictional Quality-Layer Example: Contract Review Prose Polish

This example is fictional and illustrative. It uses no real client facts and
no legal citations. It shows how `legal-prose-polish` can improve draft legal
work product without changing legal meaning. Attorney review remains required.

## Scenario

A reviewer ran `skills/contracts/contract-risk-review/SKILL.md` on a
fictional vendor services agreement for Blue Harbor Foods LLC. The first draft
identified an indemnity issue but used vague, AI-sounding prose.

## Before Legal Prose Polish

Draft legal work product for attorney review. Not legal advice.

It is important to note that the indemnity provision presents a potentially
robust risk landscape for the customer. Moreover, the vendor's obligation
could be viewed as somewhat broad, and this underscores the need to delve into
the parties' respective responsibilities. The provision may create issues in
connection with third-party claims, but the overall balance may still be
commercially acceptable depending on business context.

## After Legal Prose Polish

Draft legal work product for attorney review. Not legal advice.

The indemnity provision is broad. It requires the customer to indemnify the
vendor for third-party claims arising from the customer's use of the services,
but it does not include a matching vendor indemnity for the vendor's breach,
misconduct, or IP infringement.

Attorney review should confirm:

- whether the client will accept one-way indemnity
- whether vendor-caused claims should be carved out
- whether the agreement should include an IP infringement indemnity
- whether any insurance requirement supports the indemnity allocation

## Preservation Notes

The polish pass preserved the risk finding and attorney-review warning. It
removed filler and vague balancing language, made responsibility clearer, and
left business and legal decisions for attorney review.
