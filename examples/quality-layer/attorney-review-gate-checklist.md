# Fictional Quality-Layer Example: Attorney Review Gate Checklist

This example is fictional and illustrative. It uses no real client facts and
no legal citations. It shows the shape of an `attorney-review-gate` checklist
for a high-risk draft. A ready status means ready for attorney review, not
ready for reliance, sending, filing, or signature.

## Scenario

A fictional employment team prepared a draft termination-risk summary for
Harbor Kite Software Inc. before a planned employee termination in California.

## Attorney Review Gate

Status: Not ready for attorney review.

Reasons:

- Key facts about protected activity and leave history are missing.
- California final-pay timing has not been verified by counsel.
- The draft contains manager comments that may reveal privileged legal advice
  if sent outside the legal team.
- The draft makes a low-risk conclusion without resolving conflicting source
  documents.

## Must-Confirm Facts

- Employee tenure, role, compensation, and exemption status.
- Proposed termination date and whether the employee is remote or onsite.
- Performance warnings, complaint history, leave requests, accommodation
  requests, and retaliation-risk facts.
- Whether any manager notes conflict with HR records.

## Must-Confirm Law

- California final-pay and wage-statement timing.
- Any applicable protected-leave, accommodation, retaliation, or notice rules.
- Any policy, handbook, offer-letter, or agreement requirements.

## Must-Confirm Citations And Sources

- No legal authorities were supplied. Insert `[ATTORNEY TO VERIFY LAW]` where
  the draft refers to legal requirements.
- Source-check HR records against manager notes before relying on the factual
  chronology.

## Client Decision Points

- Whether to delay termination pending more documentation.
- Whether to offer separation terms.
- Whether the communication should come from HR, counsel, or a manager.

## Privilege And Confidentiality Flags

- Keep legal risk analysis inside privileged channels.
- Prepare a separate external-facing talking-points version without legal
  advice, risk scoring, or counsel mental impressions.
