# Fictional Quality-Layer Example: Research Memo Source Validation

This example is fictional and illustrative. It uses no real client facts and
no legal citations. It shows the shape of a `source-validation` claim table.
It does not verify current law. Attorney review remains required.

## Scenario

A fictional research memo asks whether a proposed customer-notice process is
likely to satisfy a new state privacy rule. The only materials available in
the session are a fictional agency FAQ, a fictional draft policy, and a
fictional product workflow summary.

## Claim Table After Source Validation

| Claim | Source status | Available source | Issue | Recommended revision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| The product workflow sends notice before account activation. | Source-supported | Product workflow summary, section 2 | None from available materials. | Keep, with source reference. |
| The draft policy describes the data categories collected. | Source-supported | Draft policy, section 1 | None from available materials. | Keep, with source reference. |
| The state privacy rule requires notice before collection. | Legal authority required | No rule text supplied | The agency FAQ mentions notice timing but is not enough to state the rule. | Replace with `[ATTORNEY TO VERIFY RULE TEXT AND TIMING REQUIREMENT]`. |
| The process likely satisfies the rule. | Attorney judgment required | Agency FAQ and draft policy | The conclusion depends on current law and legal interpretation. | Reframe as an issue for counsel, not a conclusion. |
| No consent is required. | Unsupported | No source supplied | The memo gives no authority or source for consent analysis. | Remove or mark `[VERIFY CONSENT REQUIREMENT]`. |

## Unsupported Claims List

- "No consent is required."

## Contradictions List

- None identified from the available fictional materials.

## Attorney Verification Checklist

- Verify the current rule text and effective date.
- Confirm whether the agency FAQ is authoritative or only guidance.
- Confirm whether notice timing differs by data category or user type.
- Confirm whether consent, opt-out, or separate disclosure obligations apply.
