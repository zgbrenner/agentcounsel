# Sample Request — restructuring-term-sheet-review

> Fictional illustration. All parties, facts, figures, and documents below are
> invented for this example.

Please run **restructuring-term-sheet-review** on a draft forbearance agreement
and flag the issues for counsel.

**Matter facts**

- Our client: Birchwood Logistics Inc., the borrower.
- Counterparty: Cardinal Credit Partners, the lender.
- The user's role: in-house counsel for Birchwood (debtor-side / borrower).
- Document: a draft Forbearance Agreement (fictional) following a payment
  default under a term loan.
- Transaction type: out-of-court forbearance and workout.

**Materials provided**

- Draft Forbearance Agreement (fictional), with provisions at:
  - §2 "Forbearance Period" — references a period tied to milestones.
  - §4 "Milestones" — lists milestones; some dates are marked "TBD".
  - §6 "Releases" — a release in favor of the lender.
  - §8 "Additional Collateral" — references new collateral to be granted.
  - §11 "Fees" — a forbearance fee.
  - §13 "Termination Events" — lists termination events.

**Stated facts**

- Birchwood wants to understand the key terms, the issues from the borrower's
  side, and where to focus negotiation.
- A confidentiality provision is expected but Birchwood has not located one in
  the draft.

Please: build a key terms table and an issue list with a risk matrix from the
borrower's perspective; cite section numbers; treat any "TBD" date as
`not provided`; give negotiation points as direction only; do **not** conclude
enforceability or legal sufficiency, and do **not** calculate any deadline.
