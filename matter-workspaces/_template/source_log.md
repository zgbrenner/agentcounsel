# Source Log

> Draft work product for attorney review. Record every source consulted for this
> matter and classify how well it supports the work that relies on it. This log
> feeds `skills/legal-methodology/source-validation/SKILL.md`. It organizes and
> labels sources — it does **not** perform automated legal verification. Every
> authority and deadline still requires attorney verification.

## Classification Vocabulary

Use these exact labels in the **Classification** column (they match the
source-validation and citation-integrity quality checks):

- `source-supported` — directly supported by this source.
- `source-mentioned but insufficient` — touched on but not adequately supported.
- `unsupported` — no source supports the point.
- `contradicted by source` — a source cuts against the point.
- `legal authority required` — a legal proposition needing a verified citation.
- `attorney judgment required` — only the attorney can resolve it.
- `user-provided authority` — supplied by the user (still verify).
- `model-suggested authority requiring verification` — surfaced by the model, not
  yet verified against an authoritative source.
- `unsupported or unverifiable authority` — could not be located or verified.
